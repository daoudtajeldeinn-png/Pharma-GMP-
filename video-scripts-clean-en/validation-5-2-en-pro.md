Video Script: Validation - Unit 2 Revalidation Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss Revalidation.
Revalidation is essential to ensure continuity of validation state after changes or over time.
What is Revalidation?
(1:00 - 5:00) Revalidation is the re-verification of the system or process after changes or over time.
Re-verification of the system Re-verification of the process Re-verification of equipment Ensure quality continuity of Revalidation: Ensures continuity of validation state Ensures changes do not have negative effects Ensures regulatory compliance Essential for continuous quality Revalidation is essential after significant changes Revalidation is essential periodically Revalidation must be documented When is Revalidation Required?
(5:00 - 10:00) There are several situations that require revalidation.
Main Situations: After Significant Changes: Equipment change Process change Raw material change Site change After a Specific Time Period: Periodic revalidation Usually every 1-3 years Depends on system type Depends on usage After Problems: After deviations After quality failures After complaints After recalls After Improvements: After process improvement After equipment update After software update After procedure update Types of Revalidation (10:00 - 14:00) There are several types of revalidation, each serving a different purpose.
Full Revalidation: Re-execute all validation stages Essential after large changes Requires DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ Costly but comprehensive Partial Revalidation: Re-execute specific stages Essential after small changes Requires only affected stages Less costly and faster Periodic Revalidation: Re-validate periodically Essential to maintain validation state Based on risk assessment Usually every 1-3 years Continued Process Verification: Continuous performance monitoring Essential for continuous processes Requires monitoring and analysis Part of CPV Revalidation Procedures (14:00 - 18:00) Executing revalidation requires following specific procedures.
Steps: Need Assessment: Evaluate if revalidation is required Determine type of revalidation required Define scope Identify resources Plan Development: Develop revalidation plan Define required stages Define timeline Identify resources Revalidation Execution: Execute required stages Collect data Analyze results Address any problems Documentation: Document all activities Prepare revalidation report Review report Approve report Validation Status Update: Update validation status Update records Notify employees Notify regulatory authorities when necessary (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is Revalidation When is Revalidation required Types of Revalidation Revalidation procedures Key Points to Remember: Revalidation is essential for validation continuity Revalidation is required after changes Revalidation is required periodically Documentation is essential for review In the next lecture, we will discuss Validation Maintenance.
