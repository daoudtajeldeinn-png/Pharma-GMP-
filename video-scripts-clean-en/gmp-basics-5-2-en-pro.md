Validation is essential to ensure that processes, equipment, and systems operate correctly and consistently.
What is Validation?
(1:00 - 5:00) Validation is the process of demonstrating that any process, system, or activity consistently produces the required results.
Demonstrating with evidence that the process produces required results Ensuring consistency and quality Regulatory compliance Risk reduction Types of Validation: Equipment Validation: Proving equipment operates correctly Process Validation: Proving the process produces good product Cleaning Validation: Proving cleaning is effective Analytical Method Validation: Proving analytical methods are accurate Validation requires scientific evidence Validation must be documented Validation must be reviewed regularly Validation Principles (5:00 - 9:00) Validation must follow specific principles.
Planning: Planning validation before starting Execution: Organized and controlled execution Documentation: Complete documentation of activities Review: Reviewing results Maintenance: Continuous maintenance of validated state Planning determines scope and approach Execution follows approved protocols Documentation provides compliance evidence Review ensures effectiveness Maintenance ensures quality continuity Validation Stages (9:00 - 14:00) Validation includes several organized stages.
Planning: Determine scope Determine team Develop protocol Approve protocol Execution: Execute protocol Collect data Monitor activities Address problems Reporting: Analyze data Prepare report Review report Approve report Maintenance: Monitor performance Re-validate when necessary Continuous updates Documentation Planning must be comprehensive Execution must follow protocol Reporting must be accurate Maintenance is essential for quality continuity Validation Examples (14:00 - 18:00) Let's look at some practical examples of validation.
