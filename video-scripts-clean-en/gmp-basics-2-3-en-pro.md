Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3 Cleaning and Sterilization Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss cleaning and sterilization.
Cleaning and sterilization are essential to prevent contamination and ensure product quality.
Cleaning Principles (1:00 - 5:00) Cleaning is the removal of visible and invisible contaminants from equipment and facilities.
Types of Contaminants: Chemical Contaminants: Residual chemicals Biological Contaminants: Bacteria and fungi Physical Contaminants: Dust and particles Residual Contaminants: Residues from previous products Cleaning Principles: Cleaning must be systematic Use appropriate cleaning agents Follow documented procedures Verify cleaning effectiveness Cleaning must be performed after each use Cleaning agents must be suitable for equipment Procedures must be documented in SOPs Cleaning Methods (5:00 - 9:00) There are several cleaning methods, and choosing the appropriate method depends on the equipment and product.
Main Methods: Manual Cleaning: Using brushes and cleaning agents Automated Cleaning: Using CIP (Clean-in-Place) systems Foam Cleaning: Using foam for cleaning Ultrasonic Cleaning: For small and delicate parts for Each Method: Manual Cleaning: Suitable for equipment that cannot be cleaned automatically Requires employee training Must have clear procedures Automated Cleaning (CIP): Suitable for pipes and tanks More consistent and less error-prone Requires regular system maintenance Foam Cleaning: Suitable for large surfaces Reduces water usage Takes longer time Ultrasonic Cleaning: Suitable for small parts Effective in removing fine contaminants Requires careful monitoring Cleaning Agents (9:00 - 12:00) Selecting appropriate cleaning agents is essential for cleaning effectiveness.
Types of Cleaning Agents: Alkaline Agents: To remove fats and oils Acidic Agents: To remove mineral deposits Solvent Agents: To remove organic contaminants Oxidizing Agents: For sterilization Alkaline agents such as caustic soda Acidic agents such as nitric acid Solvent agents such as ethanol Oxidizing agents such as hydrogen peroxide Considerations for Selecting Cleaning Agents: Effectiveness against contaminants Compatibility with equipment Ease of rinsing Safety for employees Cleaning Effectiveness Verification (12:00 - 15:00) Verifying cleaning effectiveness is essential to ensure no residual contaminants remain.
Verification Methods: Visual Inspection: Verify no visible residues TOC Test: Total Organic Carbon Conductivity Test: To detect ionic residues pH Test: To detect acidic or alkaline residues Bacteria Test: To detect biological contamination Visual inspection is the first step TOC detects organic residues Conductivity detects ionic residues Bacteria test is essential for clean areas Sterilization (15:00 - 18:00) Sterilization is the removal or killing of all living microorganisms.
Sterilization Methods: Dry Heat Sterilization: Using autoclave Steam Sterilization: Using saturated steam Chemical Sterilization: Using chemical agents Radiation Sterilization: Using gamma rays Filtration Sterilization: Using HEPA filters for Each Method: Dry Heat Sterilization: Suitable for equipment that cannot withstand moisture Requires high temperature (160-180°C) Longer time than steam sterilization Steam Sterilization: Most commonly used in pharmaceutical industry Temperature 121°C for 15-30 minutes Effective against all living organisms Chemical Sterilization: Suitable for equipment that cannot withstand heat Uses agents such as ethylene oxide Requires longer ventilation time Radiation Sterilization: Suitable for pre-sterilized materials Effective against all living organisms Requires special facilities Filtration Sterilization: Suitable for liquids and gases Uses HEPA filters Effective against particles and bacteria (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: Cleaning principles Different cleaning methods Cleaning agents Cleaning effectiveness verification Sterilization methods Key Points to Remember: Cleaning is essential to prevent contamination Choosing the appropriate method depends on equipment Verifying effectiveness is essential Sterilization complements cleaning Records are essential for review In the next lecture, we will discuss personnel and their training.
