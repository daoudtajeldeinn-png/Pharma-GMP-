Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 2 Document Management Duration: 20 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss document management.
Good document management is essential to ensure documents are up-to-date, accessible, and traceable.
Document Management Principles (1:00 - 5:00) Document management encompasses all aspects of the document lifecycle from creation to disposal.
Document Control: Ensuring documents are current and compliant Traceability: Tracking all changes Access: Ensuring easy access to documents Security: Protecting documents from damage or tampering Retention: Retaining documents for the required period Every document must have a unique number Every document must have a version Every change must be documented Old documents must be archived Document Lifecycle (5:00 - 10:00) The document lifecycle includes several stages from creation to disposal.
Creation: Drafting the document Content review Initial approval Review and Approval: Review by experts Review by quality Final approval Issuance: Distributing the document Training employees Implementing the document Use: Using the document in daily work Monitoring effectiveness Collecting feedback Update: Periodic review Content update Re-approval Disposal: Archiving the document Retaining for required period Safe disposal Change Control (10:00 - 14:00) Change control is essential to ensure documents are current and compliant.
Change Control Change Request: Requesting document modification Change Evaluation: Evaluating change impact Change Approval: Approving the modification Change Implementation: Implementing the modification Communication: Informing employees of the change Training: Training employees on the change Change Request: Change request form Reason for change Proposed changes Change Evaluation: Evaluating impact on quality Evaluating impact on compliance Evaluating impact on operations Change Approval: Approval from quality Approval from management Approval from experts Change Implementation: Updating the document Reviewing approval Issuing updated document Communication: Informing all affected employees Explaining changes Explaining effective date Training: Training on changes Evaluating understanding Documentation Document Numbering System (14:00 - 17:00) A document numbering system is essential to track and organize documents.
Document Number Elements: Department Code: Identifies the department or function Document Type: Identifies document type (SOP, FORM, etc.) Serial Number: Unique number for the document Version Number: Identifies document version Example: SOP-QC-001-01 SOP = Standard Operating Procedure QC = Quality Control department 001 = Serial number 01 = First version Each department has a unique code Each document type has a unique code Serial numbers are assigned sequentially Version increases with each update Document Retention (17:00 - 20:00) Document retention is essential for review and regulatory compliance.
Retention Periods: Active Documents: Throughout usage period Obsolete Documents: 1-5 years after discontinuation Records: 5-25 years depending on type Regulatory Documents: Up to 30 years Retention Archive centrally Easy access Protection from damage Good indexing Batch records: 1-5 years Training records: Employment duration + 5 years Maintenance records: Equipment lifetime + 5 years Inspection records: 5-10 years (20:00) In this lecture, we learned: Document management principles Document lifecycle Change control Document numbering system Document retention Key Points to Remember: Document management is essential for GMP Document lifecycle includes several stages Change control is essential Numbering system is essential for tracking Retention is essential for review In the next lecture, we will discuss batch records (BMR/BPR).
