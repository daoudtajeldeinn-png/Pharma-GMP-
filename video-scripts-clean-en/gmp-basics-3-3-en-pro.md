Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3 Behavior in Clean Areas Duration: 15 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss behavior in clean areas.
Proper behavior is essential to prevent contamination and ensure product quality.
Principles of Behavior in Clean Areas (1:00 - 4:00) Clean areas require special behavior to maintain cleanliness and prevent contamination.
Minimize Movement: Excessive movement increases contamination Minimize Talking: Talking releases particles into the air Avoid Touching: Direct touch transfers bacteria Follow Adherence to all procedures Report Problems: Report any deviations Rapid movement increases air currents Loud talking releases more particles Direct touch of product or equipment transfers contamination Procedures exist for a reason Entering Clean Areas (4:00 - 7:00) Clean area entry procedures are essential to prevent introducing contamination.
Entry Grey Room: Changing room White Room: Transition area Clean Room: Manufacturing area Detailed Steps: In Grey Room: Remove outer clothing Don clean workwear Wash hands Don gloves In White Room: Don head cover Don face mask Don gown Verify all clothing is worn In Clean Room: Enter slowly Avoid rapid movements Stay in designated path Behavior During Work (7:00 - 11:00) Proper behavior during work is essential to prevent contamination.
Correct Behaviors: Slow Movement: Avoid rapid and sudden movements Minimize Talking: Talk only when necessary Avoid Touching: Do not touch face or hair Proper Breathing: Avoid breathing directly on product Use Tools: Use appropriate tools Incorrect Behaviors: Rapid movement Loud talking Touching face or hair Sneezing or coughing without covering Using mobile phone Rapid movement increases air currents Talking releases particles into the air Touching face transfers bacteria to hands Mobile phone is a contamination source Emergency Procedures (11:00 - 15:00) Emergency procedures are essential for handling unexpected situations.
Types of Emergencies: Object Drop: Dropping a tool or product Clothing Tear: Tearing workwear Injury: Employee injury Obvious Contamination: Obvious contamination of product or equipment Equipment Failure: Sudden equipment failure Handling Object Drop: Report immediately Do not touch the object Follow cleaning procedures Document Clothing Tear: Leave area immediately Change clothing Re-enter with proper procedures Document Injury: Leave area immediately Seek medical assistance Document Investigate cause Obvious Contamination: Report immediately Isolate affected product Follow cleaning procedures Investigate cause Equipment Failure: Stop equipment immediately Report failure Document Investigate cause (15:00) In this lecture, we learned: Principles of behavior in clean areas Clean area entry procedures Behavior during work Emergency procedures Key Points to Remember: Minimizing movement and talking is essential Entry procedures must be followed precisely Proper behavior during work is essential Emergency procedures are essential In the next lecture, we will discuss documents and their types.
