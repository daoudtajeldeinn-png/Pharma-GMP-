Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 2 Equipment and Maintenance Duration: 22 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss equipment and its maintenance.
Proper equipment and good maintenance are essential to ensure product quality and consistency.
Equipment Selection (1:00 - 5:00) Selecting appropriate equipment is the first step to ensure quality.
Key Selection Criteria: Suitability for Purpose: Equipment must suit the required process Ease of Cleaning: Design must facilitate cleaning and sterilization Materials: Materials must be non-reactive with the product Accuracy: Equipment must provide required accuracy Compliance: Equipment must comply with GMP standards Stainless Steel is the most commonly used material Surfaces must be non-porous Corners must be rounded to facilitate cleaning Equipment must be qualifiable Equipment Qualification (5:00 - 9:00) Equipment Qualification is essential to ensure equipment operates correctly.
Qualification DQ (Design Qualification): Design qualification IQ (Installation Qualification): Installation qualification OQ (Operational Qualification): Operational qualification PQ (Performance Qualification): Performance qualification for Each Stage: DQ - Design Qualification: Review technical specifications Ensure design meets requirements Review materials used Ensure ease of cleaning IQ - Installation Qualification: Ensure correct installation Review drawings Verify connections Ensure regulatory compliance OQ - Operational Qualification: Test all functions Verify measurement accuracy Test safety systems Ensure correct operation PQ - Performance Qualification: Test performance under normal operating conditions Verify consistency Test performance under extreme conditions Ensure continuous quality Preventive Maintenance (9:00 - 13:00) Preventive maintenance is essential to prevent failures and ensure quality continuity.
Types of Maintenance: Preventive Maintenance: Regular maintenance Predictive Maintenance: Based on monitoring Corrective Maintenance: After failure occurs Preventive Maintenance Elements: Regular maintenance schedule Spare parts list Maintenance procedures Maintenance records Preventive maintenance reduces downtime Records are essential for review Employees must be trained on maintenance Calibration (13:00 - 16:00) Calibration is essential to ensure measurement accuracy.
Equipment Requiring Calibration: Scales Thermometers Hygrometers Pressure gauges Analytical instruments Calibration Use certified standards Regular calibration schedule Calibration records Deviation handling Calibration must be performed by certified entities Records must be kept for review Deviations must be investigated Equipment Management (16:00 - 19:00) Equipment management encompasses all aspects of equipment lifecycle.
Equipment Management Elements: Registration: Register all equipment Tracking: Track equipment location Maintenance: Maintenance schedule Calibration: Calibration schedule Retirement: Retirement procedures Equipment Records: Master equipment record Maintenance record Calibration record Qualification record Each equipment piece must have a unique number Records must be up-to-date Procedures must be documented Practical Examples (19:00 - 22:00) Let's look at some practical examples.
Example 1: Mixer: DQ: Review technical specifications IQ: Ensure correct installation OQ: Test speed and time PQ: Test mixing consistency Maintenance: Lubricate moving parts Calibration: Calibrate temperature gauge Example 2: Balance: DQ: Review required accuracy IQ: Ensure installation on level surface OQ: Test accuracy PQ: Test daily use Maintenance: Regular cleaning Calibration: Regular calibration (22:00) In this lecture, we learned: Selecting appropriate equipment Equipment qualification (DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ) Preventive maintenance Calibration Equipment management Key Points to Remember: Equipment must be suitable for purpose Qualification is essential Preventive maintenance prevents failures Calibration ensures accuracy Records are essential for review In the next lecture, we will discuss cleaning and sterilization.
