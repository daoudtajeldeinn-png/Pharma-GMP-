Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 1 Change Control Duration: 20 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss change control.
Change control is essential to ensure that any change in the process or product is done in an organized and controlled manner.
What is Change Control?
(1:00 - 5:00) Change control is a system for managing all changes in the pharmaceutical industry.
An organized system for managing changes Ensures changes are safe and effective Prevents unauthorized changes Provides a record for traceability Types of Changes: Minor Changes: Small changes that do not affect quality Major Changes: Large changes that may affect quality Emergency Changes: Immediate changes due to emergencies Minor changes may include changing supplier Major changes may include changing process Emergency changes may include replacing failed equipment Change Control Principles (5:00 - 9:00) Change control must follow specific principles.
Planning: Planning the change before implementation Evaluation: Evaluating the impact of the change Approval: Obtaining approval before implementation Implementation: Implementing the change in an organized manner Documentation: Documenting all changes Review: Reviewing change effectiveness Planning reduces risks Evaluation determines potential impact Approval ensures compliance Organized implementation ensures success Documentation provides a record for traceability Review ensures effectiveness Change Control Procedures (9:00 - 15:00) Change control procedures include several organized steps.
Steps: Change Request: Submit change request form Explain reason for change Describe proposed change Identify expected benefits Change Evaluation: Evaluate impact on quality Evaluate impact on compliance Evaluate impact on operations Evaluate impact on cost Change Approval: Approval from quality Approval from management Approval from experts Approval from regulatory authorities when necessary Implementation Planning: Determine implementation date Identify required resources Identify responsibilities Identify emergency procedures Change Implementation: Implement change according to plan Monitor implementation Record all activities Address any problems Effectiveness Verification: Verify that change is effective Verify no negative effects Verify compliance Document Change Examples (15:00 - 20:00) Let's look at some practical examples of changes.
Example 1: Supplier Change: Change Request: Changing raw material supplier Evaluation: Evaluating quality of new supplier Approval: Approval from quality and purchasing Implementation: Order from new supplier Verification: Testing new raw materials Example 2: Process Change: Change Request: Changing process temperature Evaluation: Evaluating temperature impact on quality Approval: Approval from quality and R&D Implementation: Changing temperature Verification: Testing final product Example 3: Equipment Change: Change Request: Replacing old mixer with new mixer Evaluation: Evaluating new mixer specifications Approval: Approval from quality and maintenance Implementation: Installing new mixer Verification: Qualifying new mixer (20:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is change control Change control principles Change control procedures Change examples Key Points to Remember: Change control is essential for GMP Changes must be planned and evaluated Approval is necessary before implementation Effectiveness verification is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss validation.
