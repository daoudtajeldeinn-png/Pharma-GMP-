What is CAPA?

(1:00 - 5:00) CAPA stands for Corrective and Preventive Actions.

Corrective Actions: Actions to correct current problems Preventive Actions: Actions to prevent future problems Organized system for problem management Essential for regulatory compliance of CAPA: Prevents problem recurrence Improves quality Ensures regulatory compliance Reduces costs CAPA is an essential part of GMP CAPA is required by all regulatory authorities CAPA is essential for patient safety CAPA is essential for product quality Difference Between Corrective and Preventive Actions (5:00 - 9:00) It is important to understand the difference between corrective and preventive actions.

Corrective Actions: Taken after the problem occurs Aim to correct the current problem Aim to prevent problem recurrence Executed after investigating the problem Preventive Actions: Taken before the problem occurs Aim to prevent potential problems Executed based on risk analysis Executed based on historical data Corrective actions are a reaction to problems Preventive actions are proactive Both are essential Both must be documented When is CAPA Required?

(9:00 - 13:00) There are several situations that require CAPA implementation.

Main Situations: Deviations: Deviations from standards Deviations from procedures Deviations from specifications Deviations from regulations Complaints: Customer complaints Patient complaints Doctor complaints Pharmacist complaints Recalls: Product recalls from market Defective product recalls Unsafe product recalls Ineffective product recalls Inspections: Internal inspection results External inspection results Regulatory inspection results Supplier inspection results Data Analysis: Negative trend analysis Historical data analysis Performance metrics analysis Risk analysis Regulatory Requirements (13:00 - 15:00) CAPA is required by various regulatory requirements.
