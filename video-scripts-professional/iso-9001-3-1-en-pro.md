Video Script: ISO 9001 - Unit 1 Leadership Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss leadership in the quality management system.

Leadership is essential for the success of the quality management system.

Role of Top Management (1:00 - 6:00) Top management has a vital role in the quality management system.

Main Responsibilities: Commitment to Quality: Top management must be committed to quality Policy Top management must define the quality policy Resource Provision: Top management must provide resources Encouragement of Improvement: Top management must encourage continuous improvement Communication: Top management must communicate effectively Top management sets organizational culture Top management sets direction Top management provides support Top management encourages participation Quality Policy (6:00 - 11:00) Quality policy is a statement from top management clarifying its commitment to quality.

Quality Policy Elements: Commitment: Commitment to quality and compliance Objectives: Main quality objectives Customers: Focus on customers Improvement: Commitment to continuous improvement Alignment: Alignment with organization policy Quality Policy Requirements: Must be appropriate for the organization Must be clear and understandable Must be measurable Must be achievable Must be communicated to all employees Quality policy must be written Quality policy must be signed Quality policy must be reviewed regularly Quality policy must be updated when necessary Roles and Responsibilities (11:00 - 15:00) Defining roles and responsibilities is essential for the success of the quality management system.

Main Roles: Top Management: Leadership and direction Quality Manager: Management of quality management system Department Managers: Implementing quality in their departments Employees: Implementing quality procedures Top Management Representative: Coordination and monitoring Main Responsibilities: Define responsibilities clearly Document responsibilities Train employees on their responsibilities Monitor performance Review responsibilities regularly Each person must know their role Each person must know their responsibilities Each person must know how their role affects quality Each person must be responsible for the quality of their work Organizational Culture (15:00 - 18:00) Organizational culture is essential for the success of the quality management system.

Organizational Culture Elements: Values: Core values of the organization Beliefs: Beliefs about quality Behaviors: Expected behaviors Practices: Daily practices Symbols: Symbols and traditions Developing Quality Culture: Leadership must lead by example Continuous communication about quality Continuous training Reward good performance Learn from mistakes (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: Role of top management Quality policy Roles and responsibilities Organizational culture Key Points to Remember: Leadership is essential for success Quality policy is essential Roles and responsibilities are essential Organizational culture is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss Policy in detail.
