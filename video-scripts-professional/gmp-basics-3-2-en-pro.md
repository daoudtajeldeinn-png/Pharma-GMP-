of Health and Personal Hygiene (1:00 - 4:00) Personnel are the primary source of contamination in the manufacturing environment.

Good health and personal hygiene are essential to prevent this contamination.

Sources of Contamination from Personnel: Skin and hair Clothing Respiratory secretions Sudden movements Bad habits Personnel must be healthy Personnel must be clean Personnel must follow hygiene procedures Personnel must report any health problems Health Examinations (4:00 - 7:00) Health examinations are essential to ensure personnel do not pose a risk to the product.

Types of Examinations: Initial Medical Examination: Before employment Periodic Examinations: Annually or according to policy Special Examinations: When necessary What is Examined: Infectious diseases Allergies Skin diseases General health condition Examinations must be performed by a certified physician Medical records must be kept confidential Personnel must report any changes in their health condition Personal Hygiene (7:00 - 11:00) Personal hygiene includes several aspects that personnel must adhere to.

Personal Hygiene Elements: Bathing: Daily bathing or before work Hair: Regular hair washing, appropriate hair cutting Nails: Keeping nails short, not using nail polish Clothing: Wearing clean clothes, changing clothes regularly Secretions: Covering mouth when coughing or sneezing for Each Element: Bathing: Daily bathing reduces bacteria on skin Use soap and water Dry skin thoroughly Hair: Long hair must be covered Hair must be clean Avoid using strongly scented hair products Nails: Long nails accumulate bacteria underneath Nail polish may chip and fall into product Artificial nails are prohibited Clothing: Undergarments must be clean Outer garments must be changed daily Avoid jewelry and accessories Secretions: Cover mouth when coughing or sneezing Use paper tissues Dispose of tissues properly Workwear (11:00 - 15:00) Specially designed workwear is essential to reduce contamination.

Types of Workwear: General Workwear: For less clean areas Clean Workwear: For clean areas Sterile Workwear: For high-cleanliness areas Workwear Elements: Head cover Face mask Gown Gloves Shoes Goggles when necessary Head Cover: Completely covers hair Prevents hair from falling into product Must be worn correctly Face Mask: Covers mouth and nose Prevents respiratory secretions Must be changed regularly Gown: Completely covers body Must be made of non-porous material Must be changed daily or when contaminated Gloves: Protect product from direct touch Must be of appropriate type Must be changed regularly Workwear Donning Wash hands Don head cover Don face mask Don gown Don gloves Don shoes (15:00) In this lecture, we learned: of health and personal hygiene Health examinations Personal hygiene Workwear Key Points to Remember: Personnel are the primary source of contamination Health examinations are essential Personal hygiene is essential Specially designed workwear is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss behavior in clean areas.
