Risk management is essential to identify, evaluate, and control risks associated with products and processes.

What is Risk Management?

(1:00 - 5:00) Risk management is an organized process to identify, evaluate, and control risks.

Identifying potential risks Evaluating probability and severity Controlling risks Monitoring control effectiveness of Risk Management: Protecting patients Protecting the company Regulatory compliance Continuous improvement Risk management is an integral part of GMP Risk management must be continuous Risk management requires participation from all employees Risk Management Principles (5:00 - 9:00) Risk management must follow specific principles.

Scientific Evaluation: Using scientific methods Transparency: Open communication Participation: Involving all stakeholders Continuous Improvement: Continuous updates Proportionality: Control proportional to risks Scientific evaluation ensures accuracy Transparency builds trust Participation ensures comprehensive perspectives Continuous improvement ensures relevance Proportionality ensures efficiency Risk Management Procedures (9:00 - 14:00) Risk management includes several organized steps.

Steps: Risk Identification: Identify all potential risks Use tools like Brainstorming Review historical data Consult experts Risk Assessment: Evaluate probability of risks occurring Evaluate severity of risks Determine risk priority Use risk matrix Risk Control: Develop control procedures Implement procedures Evaluate procedure effectiveness Update control procedures Risk Monitoring: Monitor control effectiveness Monitor new risks Update risk assessment Continuous communication Risk identification must be comprehensive Risk assessment must be objective Risk control must be effective Risk monitoring must be continuous Risk Management Tools (14:00 - 18:00) There are several tools that can be used for risk management.

Main Tools: FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis): Analysis of failure modes and effects Identify potential failure points Evaluate severity, frequency, and detection of failure Develop control procedures FTA (Fault Tree Analysis): Fault tree analysis Identify root causes Evaluate failure probability Develop preventive procedures HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points): Hazard analysis and critical control points Identify critical control points Develop critical limits Monitor critical points Risk Matrix: Risk matrix Classify risks by probability and severity Determine risk priority Guide decisions FMEA is suitable for complex processes FTA is suitable for root cause analysis HACCP is suitable for food and pharmaceutical processes Risk Matrix is suitable for quick evaluation (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is risk management Risk management principles Risk management procedures Risk management tools Key Points to Remember: Risk management is essential for GMP Risk management must be continuous There are several tools for risk management Participation from all employees is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss internal inspection.
