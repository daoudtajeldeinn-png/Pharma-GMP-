Video Script: ISO 9001 - Unit 1 Context of the Organization Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss the context of the organization.

Understanding the organization context is essential to effectively apply the quality management system.

What is Organization Context?

(1:00 - 5:00) Organization context is the environment in which the organization operates.

Internal and external environment Factors that affect the organization Factors that affect the quality management system Factors that affect the organization's ability to achieve objectives of Understanding Context: Helps identify risks and opportunities Helps identify stakeholder needs Helps define quality management system scope Helps improve performance Context includes internal and external factors Context includes positive and negative factors Context changes continuously Context must be reviewed regularly Internal Factors (5:00 - 10:00) Internal factors are factors that the organization controls.

Main Factors: Organizational Culture: Values and principles Beliefs and behaviors Management style Work environment Performance: Current performance Past performance Performance objectives Performance metrics Resources: Human resources Financial resources Technical resources Physical resources Processes: Current processes Process efficiency Process effectiveness Process integration Structure: Organizational structure Roles and responsibilities Reporting lines Work relationships External Factors (10:00 - 14:00) External factors are factors that the organization does not control.

Main Factors: Legal Environment: Regulations and laws Regulatory standards Compliance requirements Legal changes Economic Environment: Economic conditions Inflation Exchange rates Competition Technological Environment: Technological developments Innovations New technology Cyber threats Social Environment: Demographic changes Cultural changes Societal expectations Social responsibility Environmental Environment: Climate change Sustainability Environmental protection Environmental requirements Context Analysis (14:00 - 18:00) Context analysis is essential to understand factors and their impact.

Steps: Identify Factors: Identify internal factors Identify external factors Identify positive factors Identify negative factors Evaluate Impact: Evaluate impact of each factor Evaluate probability of occurrence Evaluate severity of impact Determine priorities Identify Risks and Opportunities: Identify potential risks Identify potential opportunities Evaluate risks and opportunities Develop strategies Review Context: Review context regularly Update evaluation Update strategies Document (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is organization context Internal factors External factors Context analysis Key Points to Remember: Context includes internal and external factors Understanding context is essential Context analysis is essential Continuous review is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss Stakeholders.
