Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 1 Manufacturing Environment and Facility Design Duration: 20 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss the manufacturing environment and facility design.

Proper facility design is the foundation of GMP and essential for ensuring product quality.

Facility Design Principles (1:00 - 5:00) Facility design must follow basic GMP principles.

Key Principles: Material Flow: From raw materials to finished product Personnel Flow: From clean areas to less clean areas Process Separation: Prevent cross-contamination Ease of Cleaning: Design that facilitates cleaning and sterilization Unidirectional flow reduces contamination risk Physical separation prevents mixing of different products Non-porous surfaces prevent bacterial accumulation Different Areas in the Factory (5:00 - 10:00) A pharmaceutical factory is divided into different areas based on the required cleanliness level.

Main Areas: Raw Material Area: Receiving and storing raw materials Manufacturing Area: Where the product is manufactured Packaging Area: Packaging the finished product Storage Area: Storing the finished product Laboratory Area: Quality testing Administration Area: Offices and meetings Cleanliness Levels: Grade A: High-risk areas (such as aseptic filling) Grade B: Aseptic filling areas Grade C: Less sensitive manufacturing areas Grade D: Storage areas and general areas HVAC System (10:00 - 14:00) HVAC is the heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system, vital for controlling the manufacturing environment.

HVAC Functions: Temperature Control: Maintaining required temperature Humidity Control: Maintaining appropriate humidity level Filtration: Removing particles and microorganisms Pressure: Maintaining positive pressure in clean areas HEPA filters remove 97% of particles Positive pressure prevents contaminated air entry Continuous air change reduces contamination Water and Utilities (14:00 - 17:00) Water and other utilities are essential for pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Types of Water: Drinking Water: For general use Purified Water: For general manufacturing Water for Injection (WFI): For sterile products Reverse Osmosis (RO) Water: For specific processes Other Utilities: Steam for sterilization Compressed gas for processes Backup electricity Water must be tested regularly Pipes must be designed to facilitate cleaning Distribution system must prevent contamination Drainage System (17:00 - 20:00) The drainage system is essential to prevent contamination.

Key Principles: Drain Separation: Separating sanitary drainage from industrial drainage Treatment: Treating drainage before disposal Monitoring: Regularly monitoring drainage quality Compliance: Compliance with environmental regulations Pharmaceutical drainage may contain hazardous chemicals Treatment is necessary to protect the environment Records must be kept for review (20:00) In this lecture, we learned: Facility design principles Different areas in the factory HVAC system Water and utilities Drainage system Key Points to Remember: Unidirectional flow is essential HVAC is essential for environmental control Water must be of the appropriate type Drainage must be treated In the next lecture, we will discuss equipment and its maintenance.
