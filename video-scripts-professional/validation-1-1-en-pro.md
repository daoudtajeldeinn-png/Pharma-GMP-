What is Validation?

(1:00 - 5:00) Validation is the process of demonstrating with evidence that any process, system, or activity consistently and reliably produces the required results.

Demonstration with scientific evidence Ensuring consistency and quality Regulatory compliance Risk reduction Difference Between Verification and Validation: Verification: Did we build the system correctly?

Validation: Did we build the right system?

Verification ensures the system is built according to specifications Validation ensures the system achieves the intended purpose Both are essential for quality Types of Validation (5:00 - 10:00) There are several types of validation in the pharmaceutical industry, each serving a different purpose.

Prospective Validation: Performed before starting commercial production Used for new processes and equipment Requires detailed protocol Requires execution of 3 successful batches Concurrent Validation: Performed during commercial production Used when production cannot be stopped Requires intensive monitoring Requires contingency procedures Retrospective Validation: Performed after commercial production Used for existing processes Relies on historical data Requires comprehensive data analysis Revalidation: Performed after significant changes Includes evaluation of changes May require execution of new batches Essential to maintain validated state Validation Principles (10:00 - 14:00) Validation must follow specific principles to ensure effectiveness.

Planning: Comprehensive planning before starting Execution: Execution according to protocol Documentation: Complete documentation of activities Review: Reviewing results Maintenance: Continuous maintenance of validated state Planning determines scope and approach Execution follows approved protocol Documentation provides compliance evidence Review ensures effectiveness Maintenance ensures quality continuity Regulatory Requirements (14:00 - 18:00) Validation is required by various regulatory authorities.
