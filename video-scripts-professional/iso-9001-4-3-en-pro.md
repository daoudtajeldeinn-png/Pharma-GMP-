What are Quality Objectives?

(1:00 - 5:00) Quality objectives are specific objectives to improve product and service quality.

Measurable objectives Achievable objectives Time-bound objectives Responsibility-defined objectives of Quality Objectives: Provide clear direction Provide basis for measurement Provide incentive for improvement Provide basis for accountability Objectives must be SMART Objectives must be aligned with policy Objectives must be aligned with strategy Objectives must be communicated to all employees SMART Criteria (5:00 - 9:00) Good objectives must follow SMART criteria.

SMART Criteria: S - Specific: Objectives must be clearly defined Objectives must be clear and understandable Objectives must not be vague M - Measurable: Objectives must be measurable Objectives must have clear metrics Objectives must be trackable A - Achievable: Objectives must be realistic Objectives must be achievable Objectives must be aligned with resources R - Relevant: Objectives must be important Objectives must be aligned with policy Objectives must be aligned with strategy T - Time-bound: Objectives must have an end date Objectives must be time-bound Objectives must have a timeline Types of Quality Objectives (9:00 - 13:00) There are several types of quality objectives.

Product Quality Objectives: Improve product quality Reduce defects Reduce complaints Increase customer satisfaction Process Quality Objectives: Improve process efficiency Reduce waste Reduce time Reduce cost Service Quality Objectives: Improve service quality Improve response speed Improve response accuracy Improve customer satisfaction Employee Quality Objectives: Improve employee skills Improve employee training Improve employee satisfaction Reduce employee turnover Compliance Objectives: Improve regulatory compliance Reduce violations Improve auditing Improve documentation Developing Quality Objectives (13:00 - 16:00) Developing quality objectives requires following specific procedures.

Steps: Analyze Current Situation: Analyze current performance Analyze gaps Analyze needs Analyze opportunities Define Objectives: Define objectives based on analysis Define objectives based on policy Define objectives based on strategy Define objectives based on resources Apply SMART Criteria: Ensure objectives are specific Ensure objectives are measurable Ensure objectives are achievable Ensure objectives are relevant Ensure objectives are time-bound Approve Objectives: Review objectives Approval from top management Documentation Signature Monitoring and Evaluating Objectives (16:00 - 18:00) Monitoring and evaluating objectives is essential to ensure achievement.
