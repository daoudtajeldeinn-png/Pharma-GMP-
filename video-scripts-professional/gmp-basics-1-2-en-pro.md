Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 2 Main Regulatory Authorities Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will learn about the main regulatory authorities that establish and implement GMP standards.

These agencies play a vital role in ensuring the quality and safety of pharmaceuticals.

FDA - US Food and Drug Administration (1:00 - 5:00) FDA is the US Food and Drug Administration, one of the oldest and most important regulatory agencies in the world.

Established in 1906 Responsible for regulating drugs in the United States Issues GMP regulations (21 CFR Parts 210/211) Conducts inspections and monitoring FDA GMP Regulations: Part 210: Good Manufacturing Practice for drugs Part 211: Good Manufacturing Practice for finished pharmaceuticals FDA applies strict standards to: US manufacturing facilities Facilities that export to the US Companies seeking approval for new products EMA - European Medicines Agency (5:00 - 9:00) EMA is the European Medicines Agency, responsible for regulating drugs in the European Union.

Established in 1995 Headquarters in London (currently Amsterdam) Coordinates between national regulatory authorities in Europe Issues EU GMP guidelines EU GMP Guidelines: Volume 1: Good Manufacturing Practice for medicinal products Volume 2: Good Manufacturing Practice for starting materials Annexes: Specific guidelines (such as Annex 1 for sterile products) EMA focuses on: Harmonizing standards in Europe Evaluating marketing authorization applications Post-marketing surveillance of medicines WHO - World Health Organization (9:00 - 12:00) WHO is the World Health Organization, an international organization that sets GMP standards for developing countries.

Established in 1948 Headquarters in Geneva Issues international GMP guidelines Helps countries implement GMP standards WHO GMP Guidelines: GMP guidelines for active pharmaceutical ingredients GMP guidelines for starting materials GMP guidelines for sterile products WHO focuses on: Technical assistance to developing countries International standard harmonization Improving drug quality globally SFDA - Saudi Food and Drug Authority (12:00 - 15:00) SFDA is the Saudi Food and Drug Authority, responsible for regulating drugs in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

Established in 2003 Headquarters in Riyadh Issues Saudi GMP regulations Conducts inspections and monitoring SFDA GMP Regulations: Aligned with ICH and WHO standards Tailored to the Saudi environment Requires compliance from all companies SFDA focuses on: Protecting public health in Saudi Arabia Ensuring quality of imported and locally manufactured drugs Coordination with international agencies Differences Between Agencies (15:00 - 18:00) Although all these agencies aim for the same goal, there are differences: Main Differences: Geographic scope Technical details Inspection procedures Documentation requirements Similarities: All focus on patient safety All implement GMP standards All require strict compliance (18:00) In this lecture, we learned about: US FDA European EMA Global WHO Saudi SFDA Key Points to Remember: Each agency has its geographic scope All implement GMP standards Compliance is required for each agency In the next lecture, we will discuss the differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP.
