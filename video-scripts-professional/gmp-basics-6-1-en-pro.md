Internal inspection is essential to ensure compliance with GMP standards and identify areas for improvement.

What is Internal Inspection?

(1:00 - 5:00) Internal inspection is a systematic and independent examination of activities to determine the extent of compliance with requirements.

Systematic examination of activities Evaluation of GMP compliance Identification of improvement areas Providing evidence to management of Internal Inspection: Ensuring regulatory compliance Identifying problems before external inspection Improving processes Providing opportunity for continuous improvement Internal inspection must be independent Internal inspection must be objective Internal inspection must be documented Internal Inspection Principles (5:00 - 9:00) Internal inspection must follow specific principles.

Independence: The auditor must be independent Objectivity: Inspection must be objective Comprehensiveness: Inspection must be comprehensive Documentation: All activities must be documented Follow-up: Corrective actions must be followed up Independence ensures neutrality Objectivity ensures accuracy Comprehensiveness ensures completeness Documentation provides evidence Follow-up ensures effectiveness Internal Inspection Procedures (9:00 - 15:00) Internal inspection includes several organized steps.

Steps: Planning: Determine scope Determine team Develop inspection plan Notify employees Execution: Review documents Observe processes Interview employees Collect evidence Reporting: Analyze results Prepare inspection report Discuss results with management Identify corrective actions Follow-up: Monitor implementation of corrective actions Evaluate action effectiveness Re-inspect when necessary Document Planning must be detailed Execution must follow the plan Reporting must be accurate Follow-up is essential to ensure effectiveness Inspection Areas (15:00 - 20:00) Internal inspection must cover all GMP areas.

Main Areas: Facilities and Equipment: Facility design Equipment maintenance Equipment calibration Cleaning and sterilization Personnel: Training Health and hygiene Behavior in clean areas Roles and responsibilities Documents: Document management Batch records SOPs Records Processes: Manufacturing procedures Quality control Change control Risk management Quality: Quality tests Deviation management Complaints and recalls Continuous improvement Each area must be examined in detail Evidence must be collected Deviations must be documented Corrective actions must be identified (20:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is internal inspection Internal inspection principles Internal inspection procedures Inspection areas Key Points to Remember: Internal inspection is essential for GMP Internal inspection must be independent Internal inspection must be comprehensive Follow-up is essential to ensure effectiveness In the next lecture, we will discuss deviations and investigation.
