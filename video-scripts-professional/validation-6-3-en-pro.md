Studying practical cases is essential to understand how to apply validation in reality.

Case Study 1: Validation of a New Mixer (1:00 - 8:00) Let's look at a real-world case study of validation for a new mixer.

Background: Company: Medium-sized pharmaceutical company Product: Pharmaceutical tablets Equipment: 500-liter rotary mixer Objective: Validation of the new mixer Challenge: New mixer has not been tested before New process Limited time Limited resources Solution: Stage 1: Planning: Develop Validation Plan Define scope: Mixer only Define timeline: 8 weeks Define resources: Team of 3 people Stage 2: DQ: Review technical specifications Ensure required capacity Review materials used Ensure ease of cleaning Stage 3: IQ: Ensure installation on level surface Verify electrical connections Verify mechanical connections Ensure proper ventilation Stage 4: OQ: Test different speeds (50, 100, 150 RPM) Test temperature Test time Test safety systems Stage 5: PQ: Mix 3 different batches Verify consistency Test final product Ensure quality Result: Successful validation Mixer operates correctly Product meets all criteria Company obtained regulatory approval Lessons Learned: Good planning is essential Cross-functional team is beneficial Complete documentation is essential Continuous review is essential Case Study 2: Validation of a New Analytical Method (8:00 - 14:00) Let's look at a real-world case study of validation for a new analytical method.

Background: Company: Large pharmaceutical company Product: New drug Method: HPLC Objective: Validation of the analytical method Challenge: New and complex method High criteria Limited time Limited expertise Solution: Stage 1: Planning: Develop Validation Plan Define scope: Analytical method only Define timeline: 12 weeks Define resources: Team of 5 people Stage 2: Method Development: Develop analytical method Determine optimal conditions Define parameters Define procedures Stage 3: Validation: Specificity: Test distinction between target substance and impurities Linearity: Prepare 7 different concentrations Accuracy: Add known amounts of target substance Precision: Execute 6 repeated tests LOD: Calculate LOD based on 3:1 S/N LOQ: Calculate LOQ based on 10:1 S/N Robustness: Change small parameters Stage 4: Analysis: Analyze data Compare results with criteria Identify any deviations Identify any problems Stage 5: Documentation: Document all activities Prepare validation report Review report Approve report Result: Successful validation Method is accurate and reliable Company obtained regulatory approval Method is now used routinely Lessons Learned: Good planning is essential Technical expertise is essential Complete documentation is essential Continuous review is essential Case Study 3: Failed Validation (14:00 - 20:00) Let's look at a case study of a failed validation and what can be learned from it.
