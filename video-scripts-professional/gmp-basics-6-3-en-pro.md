Managing complaints and recalls is essential to protect patients and company reputation.

What Are Complaints?

(1:00 - 5:00) A complaint is any contact from patients, doctors, or pharmacists reporting a problem with the product.

Any contact reporting a problem May be from patients, doctors, or pharmacists May be about quality, safety, or efficacy Must be addressed quickly Types of Complaints: Quality Complaints: Product quality problems Safety Complaints: Product safety problems Efficacy Complaints: Product efficacy problems Other Complaints: Packaging or labeling problems Complaints must be recorded immediately Complaints must be investigated Complaints must be addressed quickly Complaint Management Principles (5:00 - 9:00) Complaint management must follow specific principles.

Rapid Response: Respond to complaints quickly Comprehensive Investigation: Investigate all complaints Complete Documentation: Document all activities Corrective Actions: Develop corrective actions when necessary Communication: Communicate with complainant Rapid response builds trust Comprehensive investigation identifies cause Documentation provides a record for traceability Corrective actions prevent recurrence Communication shows concern Complaint Management Procedures (9:00 - 14:00) Complaint management includes several organized steps.

Steps: Receipt: Record complaint immediately Assign complaint number Notify management Acknowledge receipt Initial Assessment: Evaluate complaint seriousness Determine if immediate action is required Determine responsible team Determine timeline Investigation: Collect information Review records Examine affected product Interview concerned employees Reporting: Prepare complaint report Identify root cause Identify corrective actions Approve report Response: Respond to complainant Explain actions taken Apologize when necessary Provide compensation when necessary Follow-up: Monitor action effectiveness Communicate with complainant Update procedures Document Immediate recording is essential Initial assessment determines priority Investigation must be comprehensive Response must be quick Follow-up is essential to ensure satisfaction What is a Recall?

(14:00 - 18:00) A recall is the removal of product from the market due to quality or safety problems.

Removing product from market May be voluntary or mandatory May be partial or complete Essential to protect patients Types of Recalls: Voluntary Recall: From the company itself Mandatory Recall: From regulatory authority Partial Recall: For a specific batch Complete Recall: For all batches Recall Identify affected product Notify regulatory authorities Notify distributors and pharmacists Notify patients Retrieve product Investigate cause Develop corrective actions Recall must be quick Recall must be comprehensive Recall must be documented Recall requires cooperation from all parties (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What are complaints Complaint management principles Complaint management procedures What is a recall Key Points to Remember: Complaints must be addressed quickly Comprehensive investigation is essential Recall is essential to protect patients Documentation is essential for review This completes the GMP Basics course.
