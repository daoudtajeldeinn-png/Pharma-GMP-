What is Process Validation?

(1:00 - 5:00) Process Validation is the process of demonstrating that a process produces a product that meets all quality requirements consistently.

Demonstrating that the process produces a good product Demonstrating that the process produces a consistent product Demonstrating that the process is under control Demonstrating that the process is safe of Process Validation: Ensures product quality Reduces deviations Ensures patient safety Essential for regulatory compliance Process Validation includes all critical processes Process Validation must be performed before commercial production Process Validation must be documented Process Validation Stages (5:00 - 12:00) Process Validation includes several organized stages.

Critical Process Parameters (CPPs): Parameters that affect product quality Must be monitored precisely Must be controlled Example: temperature, time, speed Critical Control Points (CCPs): Points where risks can be prevented or reduced Must be monitored precisely Must be controlled Example: heating point, cooling point CPPs and CCPs are determined by risk assessment CPPs and CCPs must be monitored continuously CPPs and CCPs must be documented Process Validation Examples (16:00 - 22:00) Let's look at some practical examples of Process Validation.
