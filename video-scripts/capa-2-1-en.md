# Video Script: CAPA - Unit 2.1
## Root Cause Analysis
**Duration:** 22 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss root cause analysis. Root cause analysis is essential to effectively solve problems and prevent their recurrence.

### What is Root Cause? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** The root cause is the fundamental cause of the problem, not just the apparent cause.

**Definition:**
- The fundamental cause of the problem
- The cause that, if removed, will prevent problem recurrence
- The cause that lies at the base of the cause chain
- The cause that requires deep analysis

**Importance of Root Cause Analysis:**
- Ensures permanent problem solution
- Prevents problem recurrence
- Reduces costs
- Improves quality

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Root cause is not always clear
- Root cause requires deep analysis
- Root cause requires analysis tools
- Root cause must be documented

### Root Cause Analysis Tools (5:00 - 12:00)
**Speaker:** There are several tools that can be used for root cause analysis.

**Main Tools:**

**1. 5 Whys:**
- Ask "why" 5 times
- Determine root cause
- Simple and fast
- Effective for simple problems

**Example:**
- Why did the problem occur?
- Why did the cause occur?
- Why did the second cause occur?
- And so on until reaching the root cause

**2. Fishbone Diagram (Ishikawa):**
- Visualize all possible causes
- Categorize causes into categories
- Effective for complex problems
- Provides comprehensive view

**Main Categories:**
- Materials
- Machines
- Manpower
- Methods
- Environment
- Measurement

**3. Pareto Analysis:**
- Identify most common causes
- Focus on main causes
- Effective for recurring problems
- Helps determine priorities

**4. Fault Tree Analysis:**
- Fault tree analysis
- Determine root causes
- Effective for technical problems
- Provides logical analysis

### Applying 5 Whys (12:00 - 16:00)
**Speaker:** Let's see how to apply the 5 Whys method in practice.

**Practical Example:**

**Problem:** Product quality failure

**Question 1:** Why did the product fail quality test?
**Answer:** Because concentration was low.

**Question 2:** Why was concentration low?
**Answer:** Because raw materials were low quality.

**Question 3:** Why were raw materials low quality?
**Answer:** Because the new supplier was not tested properly.

**Question 4:** Why was the new supplier not tested properly?
**Answer:** Because supplier verification procedures were not followed.

**Question 5:** Why were supplier verification procedures not followed?
**Answer:** Because the responsible employee was not trained adequately.

**Root Cause:** Lack of employee training on supplier verification procedures.

### Applying Fishbone Diagram (16:00 - 22:00)
**Speaker:** Let's see how to apply the Fishbone Diagram in practice.

**Practical Example:**

**Problem:** Increase in product defects

**Categories:**

**Materials:**
- Raw material quality
- Raw material contamination
- Material expiration

**Machines:**
- Equipment maintenance
- Equipment calibration
- Equipment malfunction

**Manpower:**
- Employee training
- Employee experience
- Employee fatigue

**Methods:**
- Manufacturing procedures
- Inspection procedures
- Documentation procedures

**Environment:**
- Temperature
- Humidity
- Contamination

**Measurement:**
- Measurement accuracy
- Device calibration
- Measurement frequency

**Analysis:**
- Identify possible causes in each category
- Identify most probable causes
- Determine root cause
- Develop corrective actions
