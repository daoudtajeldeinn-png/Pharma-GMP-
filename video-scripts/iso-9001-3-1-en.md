# Video Script: ISO 9001 - Unit 3.1
## Leadership
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss leadership in the quality management system. Leadership is essential for the success of the quality management system.

### Role of Top Management (1:00 - 6:00)
**Speaker:** Top management has a vital role in the quality management system.

**Main Responsibilities:**
1. **Commitment to Quality:** Top management must be committed to quality
2. **Policy Definition:** Top management must define the quality policy
3. **Resource Provision:** Top management must provide resources
4. **Encouragement of Improvement:** Top management must encourage continuous improvement
5. **Communication:** Top management must communicate effectively

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Top management sets organizational culture
- Top management sets direction
- Top management provides support
- Top management encourages participation

### Quality Policy (6:00 - 11:00)
**Speaker:** Quality policy is a statement from top management clarifying its commitment to quality.

**Quality Policy Elements:**
1. **Commitment:** Commitment to quality and compliance
2. **Objectives:** Main quality objectives
3. **Customers:** Focus on customers
4. **Improvement:** Commitment to continuous improvement
5. **Alignment:** Alignment with organization policy

**Quality Policy Requirements:**
- Must be appropriate for the organization
- Must be clear and understandable
- Must be measurable
- Must be achievable
- Must be communicated to all employees

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Quality policy must be written
- Quality policy must be signed
- Quality policy must be reviewed regularly
- Quality policy must be updated when necessary

### Roles and Responsibilities (11:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Defining roles and responsibilities is essential for the success of the quality management system.

**Main Roles:**
1. **Top Management:** Leadership and direction
2. **Quality Manager:** Management of quality management system
3. **Department Managers:** Implementing quality in their departments
4. **Employees:** Implementing quality procedures
5. **Top Management Representative:** Coordination and monitoring

**Main Responsibilities:**
- Define responsibilities clearly
- Document responsibilities
- Train employees on their responsibilities
- Monitor performance
- Review responsibilities regularly

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Each person must know their role
- Each person must know their responsibilities
- Each person must know how their role affects quality
- Each person must be responsible for the quality of their work

### Organizational Culture (15:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Organizational culture is essential for the success of the quality management system.

**Organizational Culture Elements:**
1. **Values:** Core values of the organization
2. **Beliefs:** Beliefs about quality
3. **Behaviors:** Expected behaviors
4. **Practices:** Daily practices
5. **Symbols:** Symbols and traditions

**Developing Quality Culture:**
- Leadership must lead by example
- Continuous communication about quality
- Continuous training
- Reward good performance
- Learn from mistakes

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Role of top management
- Quality policy
- Roles and responsibilities
- Organizational culture

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Leadership is essential for success
- Quality policy is essential
- Roles and responsibilities are essential
- Organizational culture is essential

In the next lecture, we will discuss Policy in detail.
