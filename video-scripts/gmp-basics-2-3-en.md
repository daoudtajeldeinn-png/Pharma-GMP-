# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 2.3
## Cleaning and Sterilization
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss cleaning and sterilization. Cleaning and sterilization are essential to prevent contamination and ensure product quality.

### Cleaning Principles (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Cleaning is the removal of visible and invisible contaminants from equipment and facilities.

**Types of Contaminants:**
1. **Chemical Contaminants:** Residual chemicals
2. **Biological Contaminants:** Bacteria and fungi
3. **Physical Contaminants:** Dust and particles
4. **Residual Contaminants:** Residues from previous products

**Cleaning Principles:**
- Cleaning must be systematic
- Use appropriate cleaning agents
- Follow documented procedures
- Verify cleaning effectiveness

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Cleaning must be performed after each use
- Cleaning agents must be suitable for equipment
- Procedures must be documented in SOPs

### Cleaning Methods (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** There are several cleaning methods, and choosing the appropriate method depends on the equipment and product.

**Main Methods:**
1. **Manual Cleaning:** Using brushes and cleaning agents
2. **Automated Cleaning:** Using CIP (Clean-in-Place) systems
3. **Foam Cleaning:** Using foam for cleaning
4. **Ultrasonic Cleaning:** For small and delicate parts

**Detailed Explanation for Each Method:**

**Manual Cleaning:**
- Suitable for equipment that cannot be cleaned automatically
- Requires employee training
- Must have clear procedures

**Automated Cleaning (CIP):**
- Suitable for pipes and tanks
- More consistent and less error-prone
- Requires regular system maintenance

**Foam Cleaning:**
- Suitable for large surfaces
- Reduces water usage
- Takes longer time

**Ultrasonic Cleaning:**
- Suitable for small parts
- Effective in removing fine contaminants
- Requires careful monitoring

### Cleaning Agents (9:00 - 12:00)
**Speaker:** Selecting appropriate cleaning agents is essential for cleaning effectiveness.

**Types of Cleaning Agents:**
1. **Alkaline Agents:** To remove fats and oils
2. **Acidic Agents:** To remove mineral deposits
3. **Solvent Agents:** To remove organic contaminants
4. **Oxidizing Agents:** For sterilization

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Alkaline agents such as caustic soda
- Acidic agents such as nitric acid
- Solvent agents such as ethanol
- Oxidizing agents such as hydrogen peroxide

**Considerations for Selecting Cleaning Agents:**
- Effectiveness against contaminants
- Compatibility with equipment
- Ease of rinsing
- Safety for employees

### Cleaning Effectiveness Verification (12:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Verifying cleaning effectiveness is essential to ensure no residual contaminants remain.

**Verification Methods:**
1. **Visual Inspection:** Verify no visible residues
2. **TOC Test:** Total Organic Carbon
3. **Conductivity Test:** To detect ionic residues
4. **pH Test:** To detect acidic or alkaline residues
5. **Bacteria Test:** To detect biological contamination

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Visual inspection is the first step
- TOC detects organic residues
- Conductivity detects ionic residues
- Bacteria test is essential for clean areas

### Sterilization (15:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Sterilization is the removal or killing of all living microorganisms.

**Sterilization Methods:**
1. **Dry Heat Sterilization:** Using autoclave
2. **Steam Sterilization:** Using saturated steam
3. **Chemical Sterilization:** Using chemical agents
4. **Radiation Sterilization:** Using gamma rays
5. **Filtration Sterilization:** Using HEPA filters

**Detailed Explanation for Each Method:**

**Dry Heat Sterilization:**
- Suitable for equipment that cannot withstand moisture
- Requires high temperature (160-180°C)
- Longer time than steam sterilization

**Steam Sterilization:**
- Most commonly used in pharmaceutical industry
- Temperature 121°C for 15-30 minutes
- Effective against all living organisms

**Chemical Sterilization:**
- Suitable for equipment that cannot withstand heat
- Uses agents such as ethylene oxide
- Requires longer ventilation time

**Radiation Sterilization:**
- Suitable for pre-sterilized materials
- Effective against all living organisms
- Requires special facilities

**Filtration Sterilization:**
- Suitable for liquids and gases
- Uses HEPA filters
- Effective against particles and bacteria

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Cleaning principles
- Different cleaning methods
- Cleaning agents
- Cleaning effectiveness verification
- Sterilization methods

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Cleaning is essential to prevent contamination
- Choosing the appropriate method depends on equipment
- Verifying effectiveness is essential
- Sterilization complements cleaning
- Records are essential for review
