Video Script: Validation - Unit 1 Change Control in Validation Duration: 18 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss Change Control in Validation.
Change Control is essential to ensure that any change in the system or process is done in an organized and controlled manner.
What is Change Control?
(1:00 - 5:00) Change Control is a system for managing all changes in systems and processes.
An organized system for managing changes Ensures changes are safe and effective Prevents unauthorized changes Provides a record for traceability of Change Control in Validation: Ensures continuity of validation state Prevents changes that affect quality Ensures regulatory compliance Provides a record for traceability Any change may affect validation state Changes must be evaluated before implementation Changes may require revalidation Types of Changes (5:00 - 9:00) There are several types of changes, each requiring a different level of review.
Minor Changes: Small changes that do not affect quality Do not require revalidation Require simple review Example: Changing supplier for non-critical materials Major Changes: Large changes that may affect quality May require revalidation Require comprehensive review Example: Changing main process Emergency Changes: Immediate changes due to emergencies Require immediate implementation Require subsequent review Example: Replacing broken equipment Temporary Changes: Temporary changes for a specific period Require review before and after Require requalification Example: Using alternative equipment temporarily Change Control Procedures (9:00 - 14:00) Change Control includes several organized steps.
Steps: Change Request: Submit change request form Explain reason for change Describe proposed change Identify expected benefits Change Evaluation: Evaluate impact on validation Evaluate impact on quality Evaluate impact on compliance Evaluate impact on processes Change Approval: Approval from quality Approval from management Approval from experts Approval from regulatory authorities when necessary Change Implementation: Implement change according to plan Monitor implementation Record all activities Address any problems Effectiveness Verification: Verify that change is effective Verify no negative effects Verify compliance Document Revalidation if Required: Determine if revalidation is required Execute revalidation Analyze results Document Change Control Examples (14:00 - 18:00) Let's look at some practical examples of Change Control.
Example 1: Supplier Change: Change Request: Change raw material supplier Evaluation: Evaluate quality of new supplier Approval: Approval from quality and purchasing Implementation: Order from new supplier Verification: Test new raw materials Revalidation: May require revalidation Example 2: Equipment Change: Change Request: Replace old mixer with new mixer Evaluation: Evaluate specifications of new mixer Approval: Approval from quality and maintenance Implementation: Install new mixer Verification: Validation of new mixer Revalidation: Required (DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ) Example 3: Process Change: Change Request: Change process temperature Evaluation: Evaluate impact of temperature on quality Approval: Approval from quality and R&D Implementation: Change temperature Verification: Test final product Revalidation: May require revalidation (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is Change Control Types of changes Change Control procedures Change Control examples Key Points to Remember: Change Control is essential for validation continuity Changes must be evaluated before implementation Changes may require revalidation Documentation is essential for traceability In the next lecture, we will discuss Revalidation.
