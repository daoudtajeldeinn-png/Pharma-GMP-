# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3.1
## Personnel and Training
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss personnel and their training. Well-trained personnel are the foundation of GMP and guarantee product quality.

### Importance of Personnel in GMP (1:00 - 4:00)
**Speaker:** Personnel are the most important element in the GMP system. Even the best equipment and facilities cannot ensure quality without trained personnel.

**Why Are Personnel Important?**
- They execute the processes
- They discover problems
- They document activities
- They maintain quality

**Basic Principles:**
- Every employee must be trained
- Training must be continuous
- Competence must be evaluated regularly
- Records must be kept

### Types of Training (4:00 - 8:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of training, each serving a different purpose.

**Main Types:**
1. **Orientation Training:** For new employees
2. **Basic GMP Training:** For all employees
3. **Job-Specific Training:** According to the job
4. **SOP Training:** For Standard Operating Procedures
5. **Advanced Training:** For experienced employees
6. **Refresher Training:** Periodic

**Detailed Explanation for Each Type:**

**Orientation Training:**
- Introduction to the company
- Introduction to GMP
- Company policies
- Safety procedures

**Basic GMP Training:**
- GMP principles
- Regulatory requirements
- Importance of quality
- Employee responsibilities

**Job-Specific Training:**
- Equipment usage
- Process execution
- Document management
- Emergency procedures

**SOP Training:**
- Reading and understanding SOPs
- Correct SOP execution
- Documentation
- Deviation handling

**Refresher Training:**
- Knowledge update
- Reviewing changes
- Skill improvement
- Competence evaluation

### Training Program (8:00 - 12:00)
**Speaker:** A good training program must be planned and organized.

**Training Program Elements:**
1. **Needs Assessment:** Determining what each employee needs
2. **Content Development:** Creating appropriate training materials
3. **Training Delivery:** Effective training delivery
4. **Effectiveness Evaluation:** Measuring training results
5. **Continuous Improvement:** Regularly updating the program

**Detailed Explanation:**

**Needs Assessment:**
- Job analysis
- Identifying required skills
- Evaluating current competence
- Identifying gaps

**Content Development:**
- Using current materials
- Including practical examples
- Using visual aids
- Ensuring ease of understanding

**Training Delivery:**
- Using qualified trainers
- Providing appropriate learning environment
- Encouraging participation
- Providing reference materials

**Effectiveness Evaluation:**
- Post-training tests
- Performance monitoring
- Supervisor feedback
- Self-evaluation

### Training Records (12:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Training records are essential for review and regulatory compliance.

**Training Record Contents:**
- Employee name
- Training date
- Training topic
- Trainer name
- Training duration
- Evaluation results
- Employee and trainer signatures

**Record Management:**
- Store records centrally
- Easy access to records
- Keep records for a specified period
- Protect records from damage

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Records must be up-to-date
- Records must be accurate
- Records must be traceable
- Records must be available for inspection

### Competence Evaluation (15:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Competence evaluation is essential to ensure employees are capable of correctly performing their tasks.

**Competence Evaluation Methods:**
1. **Direct Observation:** Monitoring employee during work
2. **Tests:** Written or practical tests
3. **Interviews:** Discussion with employee
4. **Record Review:** Reviewing performance records
5. **Peer Evaluation:** Evaluation by colleagues

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Evaluation must be fair
- Evaluation must be continuous
- Evaluation must be documented
- Evaluation must be used to improve performance

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Importance of personnel in GMP
- Different types of training
- Training program
- Training records
- Competence evaluation

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Personnel are the foundation of GMP
- Training must be continuous
- Records are essential for review
- Competence evaluation is essential

In the next lecture, we will discuss health and personal hygiene.
