# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 5.1
## Change Control
**Duration:** 20 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss change control. Change control is essential to ensure that any change in the process or product is done in an organized and controlled manner.

### What is Change Control? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Change control is a system for managing all changes in the pharmaceutical industry.

**Definition:**
- An organized system for managing changes
- Ensures changes are safe and effective
- Prevents unauthorized changes
- Provides a record for traceability

**Types of Changes:**
1. **Minor Changes:** Small changes that do not affect quality
2. **Major Changes:** Large changes that may affect quality
3. **Emergency Changes:** Immediate changes due to emergencies

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Minor changes may include changing supplier
- Major changes may include changing process
- Emergency changes may include replacing failed equipment

### Change Control Principles (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Change control must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Planning:** Planning the change before implementation
2. **Evaluation:** Evaluating the impact of the change
3. **Approval:** Obtaining approval before implementation
4. **Implementation:** Implementing the change in an organized manner
5. **Documentation:** Documenting all changes
6. **Review:** Reviewing change effectiveness

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Planning reduces risks
- Evaluation determines potential impact
- Approval ensures compliance
- Organized implementation ensures success
- Documentation provides a record for traceability
- Review ensures effectiveness

### Change Control Procedures (9:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Change control procedures include several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Change Request:**
- Submit change request form
- Explain reason for change
- Describe proposed change
- Identify expected benefits

**2. Change Evaluation:**
- Evaluate impact on quality
- Evaluate impact on compliance
- Evaluate impact on operations
- Evaluate impact on cost

**3. Change Approval:**
- Approval from quality
- Approval from management
- Approval from experts
- Approval from regulatory authorities when necessary

**4. Implementation Planning:**
- Determine implementation date
- Identify required resources
- Identify responsibilities
- Identify emergency procedures

**5. Change Implementation:**
- Implement change according to plan
- Monitor implementation
- Record all activities
- Address any problems

**6. Effectiveness Verification:**
- Verify that change is effective
- Verify no negative effects
- Verify compliance
- Document

### Change Examples (15:00 - 20:00)
**Speaker:** Let's look at some practical examples of changes.

**Example 1: Supplier Change:**
- Change Request: Changing raw material supplier
- Evaluation: Evaluating quality of new supplier
- Approval: Approval from quality and purchasing
- Implementation: Order from new supplier
- Verification: Testing new raw materials

**Example 2: Process Change:**
- Change Request: Changing process temperature
- Evaluation: Evaluating temperature impact on quality
- Approval: Approval from quality and R&D
- Implementation: Changing temperature
- Verification: Testing final product

**Example 3: Equipment Change:**
- Change Request: Replacing old mixer with new mixer
- Evaluation: Evaluating new mixer specifications
- Approval: Approval from quality and maintenance
- Implementation: Installing new mixer
- Verification: Qualifying new mixer
