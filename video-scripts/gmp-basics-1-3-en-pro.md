Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3 Differences Between GMP, GCP, and GLP Duration: 12 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will learn about the differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP.
These three practices together form the quality framework in the pharmaceutical industry, but each focuses on a different stage.
GMP - Good Manufacturing Practice (1:00 - 4:00) GMP, as we learned, focuses on the manufacturing stage.
Scope: Drug manufacturing Quality control during manufacturing Storage and distribution Facility and equipment management Objective: Ensure product quality consistency Prevent contamination and errors Ensure regulatory compliance Examples: Manufacturing tablets and capsules Filling liquid medications Storing raw materials and finished products GCP - Good Clinical Practice (4:00 - 7:00) GCP focuses on the clinical trial stage.
Scope: Designing clinical trials Conducting clinical trials Collecting and analyzing data Protecting participants' rights Objective: Ensure participant safety in trials Collect reliable data Protect human rights Examples: Phase I trials Phase II/III trials Bioavailability studies GCP includes: Ethical approval Participant selection Trial monitoring Report preparation GLP - Good Laboratory Practice (7:00 - 10:00) GLP focuses on the preclinical research and development stage.
Scope: Preclinical studies Toxicity testing Efficacy testing Sample analysis Objective: Ensure preclinical data quality Minimize laboratory errors Ensure result consistency Examples: Toxicity testing on animals Stability studies Efficacy testing in laboratory GLP includes: Laboratory management Employee training Equipment maintenance Document management Relationship Between the Three (10:00 - 12:00) GMP, GCP, and GLP complement each other.
Timeline: GLP - Preclinical research and development GCP - Clinical trials GMP - Manufacturing and marketing Integration: All ensure drug quality and safety All require documentation and compliance All aim to protect patients Drug lifecycle: Laboratory research (GLP) Clinical trials (GCP) Manufacturing (GMP) Marketing and monitoring (GMP + GCP) (12:00) In this lecture, we learned: Differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP Scope of each practice How the three integrate together Key Points to Remember: GMP = Manufacturing GCP = Clinical trials GLP = Preclinical research All are essential for drug quality In the next lecture, we will discuss the manufacturing environment and facility design.
