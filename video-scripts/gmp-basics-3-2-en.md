# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3.2
## Health and Personal Hygiene
**Duration:** 15 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss health and personal hygiene. Health and personal hygiene are essential to prevent contamination and ensure product quality.

### Importance of Health and Personal Hygiene (1:00 - 4:00)
**Speaker:** Personnel are the primary source of contamination in the manufacturing environment. Good health and personal hygiene are essential to prevent this contamination.

**Sources of Contamination from Personnel:**
- Skin and hair
- Clothing
- Respiratory secretions
- Sudden movements
- Bad habits

**Basic Principles:**
- Personnel must be healthy
- Personnel must be clean
- Personnel must follow hygiene procedures
- Personnel must report any health problems

### Health Examinations (4:00 - 7:00)
**Speaker:** Health examinations are essential to ensure personnel do not pose a risk to the product.

**Types of Examinations:**
1. **Initial Medical Examination:** Before employment
2. **Periodic Examinations:** Annually or according to policy
3. **Special Examinations:** When necessary

**What is Examined:**
- Infectious diseases
- Allergies
- Skin diseases
- General health condition

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Examinations must be performed by a certified physician
- Medical records must be kept confidential
- Personnel must report any changes in their health condition

### Personal Hygiene (7:00 - 11:00)
**Speaker:** Personal hygiene includes several aspects that personnel must adhere to.

**Personal Hygiene Elements:**
1. **Bathing:** Daily bathing or before work
2. **Hair:** Regular hair washing, appropriate hair cutting
3. **Nails:** Keeping nails short, not using nail polish
4. **Clothing:** Wearing clean clothes, changing clothes regularly
5. **Secretions:** Covering mouth when coughing or sneezing

**Detailed Explanation for Each Element:**

**Bathing:**
- Daily bathing reduces bacteria on skin
- Use soap and water
- Dry skin thoroughly

**Hair:**
- Long hair must be covered
- Hair must be clean
- Avoid using strongly scented hair products

**Nails:**
- Long nails accumulate bacteria underneath
- Nail polish may chip and fall into product
- Artificial nails are prohibited

**Clothing:**
- Undergarments must be clean
- Outer garments must be changed daily
- Avoid jewelry and accessories

**Secretions:**
- Cover mouth when coughing or sneezing
- Use paper tissues
- Dispose of tissues properly

### Workwear (11:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Specially designed workwear is essential to reduce contamination.

**Types of Workwear:**
1. **General Workwear:** For less clean areas
2. **Clean Workwear:** For clean areas
3. **Sterile Workwear:** For high-cleanliness areas

**Workwear Elements:**
- Head cover
- Face mask
- Gown
- Gloves
- Shoes
- Goggles when necessary

**Detailed Explanation:**

**Head Cover:**
- Completely covers hair
- Prevents hair from falling into product
- Must be worn correctly

**Face Mask:**
- Covers mouth and nose
- Prevents respiratory secretions
- Must be changed regularly

**Gown:**
- Completely covers body
- Must be made of non-porous material
- Must be changed daily or when contaminated

**Gloves:**
- Protect product from direct touch
- Must be of appropriate type
- Must be changed regularly

**Workwear Donning Procedures:**
1. Wash hands
2. Don head cover
3. Don face mask
4. Don gown
5. Don gloves
6. Don shoes

### Summary (15:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Importance of health and personal hygiene
- Health examinations
- Personal hygiene
- Workwear

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Personnel are the primary source of contamination
- Health examinations are essential
- Personal hygiene is essential
- Specially designed workwear is essential

In the next lecture, we will discuss behavior in clean areas.
