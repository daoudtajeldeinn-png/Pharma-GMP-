# Video Script: CAPA - Unit 2.2
## Developing Corrective Actions
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss developing corrective actions. Corrective actions are essential to solve current problems.

### Principles of Developing Corrective Actions (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Developing corrective actions must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Effectiveness:** Actions must be effective
2. **Realism:** Actions must be realistic
3. **Appropriateness:** Actions must be appropriate
4. **Timeline:** Actions must have a timeline
5. **Responsibility:** Actions must have a clear responsible person

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Actions must solve the problem
- Actions must be executable
- Actions must be appropriate for the problem
- Actions must be executed in a timely manner
- Actions must have a responsible person

### Steps for Developing Corrective Actions (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** Developing corrective actions includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Define Required Actions:**
- Define actions to correct the problem
- Define actions to prevent problem recurrence
- Define actions for improvement
- Determine priorities

**2. Develop Actions:**
- Develop detailed actions
- Define required resources
- Define timeline
- Define responsibilities

**3. Review Actions:**
- Review actions by experts
- Review actions by quality
- Review actions by management
- Required adjustments

**4. Approve Actions:**
- Approval from quality
- Approval from management
- Approval from regulatory authorities when necessary
- Final signature

### Types of Corrective Actions (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of corrective actions.

**Main Types:**

**1. Immediate Actions:**
- Immediate actions to contain the problem
- Immediate actions to prevent impact
- Immediate actions to protect patients
- Immediate actions to protect product

**2. Short-term Actions:**
- Actions for quick solution
- Actions for temporary solution
- Actions for interim solution
- Actions for immediate solution

**3. Long-term Actions:**
- Actions for permanent solution
- Actions for radical solution
- Actions for final solution
- Actions for sustainable solution

**4. Improvement Actions:**
- Actions to improve processes
- Actions to improve quality
- Actions to improve efficiency
- Actions to improve effectiveness

### Examples of Corrective Actions (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Let's look at some practical examples.

**Example 1: Product Quality Failure**

**Immediate Actions:**
- Isolate affected batch
- Stop production
- Notify management
- Notify quality

**Short-term Actions:**
- Test all affected batches
- Retest product
- Handle affected batches

**Long-term Actions:**
- Train employees
- Update procedures
- Improve process

**Example 2: Process Deviation**

**Immediate Actions:**
- Stop process
- Isolate affected product
- Notify supervisor
- Notify quality

**Short-term Actions:**
- Review process
- Correct parameters
- Retest product

**Long-term Actions:**
- Update SOPs
- Train employees
- Improve process

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Principles of developing corrective actions
- Steps for developing corrective actions
- Types of corrective actions
- Examples of corrective actions

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Corrective actions are essential
- Actions must be effective
- Actions must be realistic
- Actions must be documented
