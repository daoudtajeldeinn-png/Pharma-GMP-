# Video Script: QC Lab & ISO/IEC 17025 - Unit 2.1
## Document Management in Laboratory
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss document management in the laboratory. Document management is essential to ensure compliance and quality.

### Importance of Document Management (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Document management is essential for several vital reasons.

**Main Reasons:**
1. **Compliance:** Essential for regulatory compliance
2. **Traceability:** Track all activities and decisions
3. **Training:** Source for training and education
4. **Improvement:** Basis for continuous improvement
5. **Review:** Review performance and effectiveness

**Basic Principles:**
- If not documented, it didn't happen
- Documentation must be accurate
- Documentation must be current
- Documentation must be traceable

### Types of Documents in Laboratory (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of documents in the laboratory.

**Main Types:**

**1. SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures):**
- Standard operating procedures
- Analytical procedures
- Maintenance procedures
- Safety procedures

**2. Protocols (Analysis Protocols):**
- Analysis protocols
- Inspection protocols
- Test protocols
- Calibration protocols

**3. Records (Laboratory Records):**
- Analysis records
- Maintenance records
- Calibration records
- Training records

**4. Reports (Laboratory Reports):**
- Analysis reports
- Quality reports
- Audit reports
- CAPA reports

**5. Regulatory Documents:**
- Accreditation certificates
- Operating licenses
- Regulatory authority approvals
- Compliance documents

### SOP Management (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** SOP management is essential to ensure consistency and quality.

**Procedures:**

**1. Develop SOPs:**
- Develop comprehensive SOPs
- Develop clear SOPs
- Develop executable SOPs
- Develop understandable SOPs

**2. Review SOPs:**
- Review by experts
- Review by quality
- Review by management
- Required adjustments

**3. Approve SOPs:**
- Approval from quality
- Approval from management
- Documentation
- Signature

**4. Distribute SOPs:**
- Distribute to concerned employees
- Train employees on SOPs
- Ensure understanding
- Ensure compliance

**5. Update SOPs:**
- Periodic review
- Update when necessary
- Re-approval
- Re-distribution

### Record Management (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Record management is essential for tracking and review.

**Procedures:**

**1. Create Records:**
- Create comprehensive records
- Create clear records
- Create understandable records
- Create traceable records

**2. Fill Records:**
- Fill records in real-time
- Fill records accurately
- Fill records completely
- Fill records legibly

**3. Review Records:**
- Review records regularly
- Review records for audit
- Review records for improvement
- Review records for compliance

**4. Archive Records:**
- Archive centrally
- Easy access
- Protect from damage
- Good indexing

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Importance of document management
- Types of documents in laboratory
- SOP management
- Record management

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Document management is essential
- There are several types of documents
- SOPs are essential for consistency
- Records are essential for tracking
