# Video Script: ISO 9001 - Unit 1.1
## History of ISO 9001
**Duration:** 15 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** Welcome to this lecture. Today we will discuss the history of ISO 9001 and its evolution over the years. Understanding the history is essential to understanding the current standards.

### Beginnings of ISO 9001 (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** ISO 9001 has a rich history dating back to the mid-20th century.

**Key Points:**
- ISO (International Organization for Standardization) was founded in 1947
- ISO 9001 was first issued in 1987
- It was a response to the need for international quality standards
- It evolved over the years to adapt to changes

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Before ISO 9001, each country had its own standards
- This created difficulties in international trade
- The need for unified standards became clear
- ISO 9001 was the solution

### ISO 9001 Editions (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** ISO 9001 went through several editions, each bringing improvements.

**Main Editions:**

**ISO 9001:1987:**
- First edition
- Focused on quality control
- 3 separate standards (9001, 9002, 9003)
- Based on British standards BS 5750

**ISO 9001:1994:**
- Second edition
- Focused on quality assurance
- Maintained basic structure
- Added additional requirements

**ISO 9001:2000:**
- Third edition - major change
- Focused on quality management
- Merged the three standards into one
- Adopted process approach

**ISO 9001:2008:**
- Fourth edition
- Minor improvements
- Additional clarifications
- Improved compatibility with ISO 14001

**ISO 9001:2015:**
- Fifth edition - latest edition
- Focused on risk management
- Adopted PDCA approach
- Major structural improvements

### Major Changes in 2015 (10:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** The 2015 edition brought significant changes.

**Major Changes:**

**1. High Level Structure:**
- Unified structure with other ISO standards
- 10 main clauses
- Easy to understand and apply
- Facilitates integration with other standards

**2. Risk Management:**
- Focus on risk management
- Risk assessment required
- Risk management part of all processes
- Reduces potential problems

**3. PDCA Approach:**
- Plan
- Do
- Check
- Act
- Continuous improvement cycle

**4. Focus on Leadership:**
- Top management has greater role
- Leadership is essential for success
- Top management responsible for quality system
- Organizational culture is important

**5. Focus on Context:**
- Understanding organization context
- Understanding stakeholder needs
- Understanding risks and opportunities
- Adapting to changes

### Summary (15:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Beginnings of ISO 9001
- Different ISO 9001 editions
- Major changes in 2015

**Key Points to Remember:**
- ISO 9001 evolved over the years
- Each edition brought improvements
- 2015 edition is the latest
- Changes focus on risk management and leadership
