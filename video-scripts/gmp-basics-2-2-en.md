# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 2.2
## Equipment and Maintenance
**Duration:** 22 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss equipment and its maintenance. Proper equipment and good maintenance are essential to ensure product quality and consistency.

### Equipment Selection (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Selecting appropriate equipment is the first step to ensure quality.

**Key Selection Criteria:**
1. **Suitability for Purpose:** Equipment must suit the required process
2. **Ease of Cleaning:** Design must facilitate cleaning and sterilization
3. **Materials:** Materials must be non-reactive with the product
4. **Accuracy:** Equipment must provide required accuracy
5. **Compliance:** Equipment must comply with GMP standards

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Stainless Steel is the most commonly used material
- Surfaces must be non-porous
- Corners must be rounded to facilitate cleaning
- Equipment must be qualifiable

### Equipment Qualification (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Equipment Qualification is essential to ensure equipment operates correctly.

**Qualification Stages:**
1. **DQ (Design Qualification):** Design qualification
2. **IQ (Installation Qualification):** Installation qualification
3. **OQ (Operational Qualification):** Operational qualification
4. **PQ (Performance Qualification):** Performance qualification

**Detailed Explanation for Each Stage:**

**DQ - Design Qualification:**
- Review technical specifications
- Ensure design meets requirements
- Review materials used
- Ensure ease of cleaning

**IQ - Installation Qualification:**
- Ensure correct installation
- Review drawings
- Verify connections
- Ensure regulatory compliance

**OQ - Operational Qualification:**
- Test all functions
- Verify measurement accuracy
- Test safety systems
- Ensure correct operation

**PQ - Performance Qualification:**
- Test performance under normal operating conditions
- Verify consistency
- Test performance under extreme conditions
- Ensure continuous quality

### Preventive Maintenance (9:00 - 13:00)
**Speaker:** Preventive maintenance is essential to prevent failures and ensure quality continuity.

**Types of Maintenance:**
1. **Preventive Maintenance:** Regular maintenance
2. **Predictive Maintenance:** Based on monitoring
3. **Corrective Maintenance:** After failure occurs

**Preventive Maintenance Elements:**
- Regular maintenance schedule
- Spare parts list
- Maintenance procedures
- Maintenance records

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Preventive maintenance reduces downtime
- Records are essential for review
- Employees must be trained on maintenance

### Calibration (13:00 - 16:00)
**Speaker:** Calibration is essential to ensure measurement accuracy.

**Equipment Requiring Calibration:**
- Scales
- Thermometers
- Hygrometers
- Pressure gauges
- Analytical instruments

**Calibration Procedures:**
- Use certified standards
- Regular calibration schedule
- Calibration records
- Deviation handling

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Calibration must be performed by certified entities
- Records must be kept for review
- Deviations must be investigated

### Equipment Management (16:00 - 19:00)
**Speaker:** Equipment management encompasses all aspects of equipment lifecycle.

**Equipment Management Elements:**
1. **Registration:** Register all equipment
2. **Tracking:** Track equipment location
3. **Maintenance:** Maintenance schedule
4. **Calibration:** Calibration schedule
5. **Retirement:** Retirement procedures

**Equipment Records:**
- Master equipment record
- Maintenance record
- Calibration record
- Qualification record

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Each equipment piece must have a unique number
- Records must be up-to-date
- Procedures must be documented

### Practical Examples (19:00 - 22:00)
**Speaker:** Let's look at some practical examples.

**Example 1: Mixer:**
- DQ: Review technical specifications
- IQ: Ensure correct installation
- OQ: Test speed and time
- PQ: Test mixing consistency
- Maintenance: Lubricate moving parts
- Calibration: Calibrate temperature gauge

**Example 2: Balance:**
- DQ: Review required accuracy
- IQ: Ensure installation on level surface
- OQ: Test accuracy
- PQ: Test daily use
- Maintenance: Regular cleaning
- Calibration: Regular calibration

### Summary (22:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Selecting appropriate equipment
- Equipment qualification (DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ)
- Preventive maintenance
- Calibration
- Equipment management

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Equipment must be suitable for purpose
- Qualification is essential
- Preventive maintenance prevents failures
- Calibration ensures accuracy
- Records are essential for review
