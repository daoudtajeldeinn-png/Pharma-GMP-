# Video Script: Validation - Unit 5.1
## Change Control in Validation
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss Change Control in Validation. Change Control is essential to ensure that any change in the system or process is done in an organized and controlled manner.

### What is Change Control? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Change Control is a system for managing all changes in systems and processes.

**Definition:**
- An organized system for managing changes
- Ensures changes are safe and effective
- Prevents unauthorized changes
- Provides a record for traceability

**Importance of Change Control in Validation:**
- Ensures continuity of validation state
- Prevents changes that affect quality
- Ensures regulatory compliance
- Provides a record for traceability

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Any change may affect validation state
- Changes must be evaluated before implementation
- Changes may require revalidation

### Types of Changes (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of changes, each requiring a different level of review.

**Main Types:**

**1. Minor Changes:**
- Small changes that do not affect quality
- Do not require revalidation
- Require simple review
- Example: Changing supplier for non-critical materials

**2. Major Changes:**
- Large changes that may affect quality
- May require revalidation
- Require comprehensive review
- Example: Changing main process

**3. Emergency Changes:**
- Immediate changes due to emergencies
- Require immediate implementation
- Require subsequent review
- Example: Replacing broken equipment

**4. Temporary Changes:**
- Temporary changes for a specific period
- Require review before and after
- Require requalification
- Example: Using alternative equipment temporarily

### Change Control Procedures (9:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** Change Control includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Change Request:**
- Submit change request form
- Explain reason for change
- Describe proposed change
- Identify expected benefits

**2. Change Evaluation:**
- Evaluate impact on validation
- Evaluate impact on quality
- Evaluate impact on compliance
- Evaluate impact on processes

**3. Change Approval:**
- Approval from quality
- Approval from management
- Approval from experts
- Approval from regulatory authorities when necessary

**4. Change Implementation:**
- Implement change according to plan
- Monitor implementation
- Record all activities
- Address any problems

**5. Effectiveness Verification:**
- Verify that change is effective
- Verify no negative effects
- Verify compliance
- Document

**6. Revalidation if Required:**
- Determine if revalidation is required
- Execute revalidation
- Analyze results
- Document

### Change Control Examples (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Let's look at some practical examples of Change Control.

**Example 1: Supplier Change:**
- Change Request: Change raw material supplier
- Evaluation: Evaluate quality of new supplier
- Approval: Approval from quality and purchasing
- Implementation: Order from new supplier
- Verification: Test new raw materials
- Revalidation: May require revalidation

**Example 2: Equipment Change:**
- Change Request: Replace old mixer with new mixer
- Evaluation: Evaluate specifications of new mixer
- Approval: Approval from quality and maintenance
- Implementation: Install new mixer
- Verification: Validation of new mixer
- Revalidation: Required (DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ)

**Example 3: Process Change:**
- Change Request: Change process temperature
- Evaluation: Evaluate impact of temperature on quality
- Approval: Approval from quality and R&D
- Implementation: Change temperature
- Verification: Test final product
- Revalidation: May require revalidation
