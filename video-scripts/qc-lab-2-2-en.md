# Video Script: QC Lab & ISO/IEC 17025 - Unit 2.2
## Sample Management
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss sample management in the laboratory. Sample management is essential to ensure result accuracy.

### Sample Management Principles (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Sample management must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Traceability:** Track all samples
2. **Storage:** Store samples correctly
3. **Preservation:** Preserve samples from damage
4. **Safety:** Ensure sample safety
5. **Documentation:** Document all activities

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Samples must be tracked from receipt to disposal
- Samples must be stored in appropriate conditions
- Samples must be preserved from damage
- Samples must be handled safely
- All activities must be documented

### Sample Receipt Procedures (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Sample receipt must follow specific procedures.

**Procedures:**

**1. Receipt:**
- Receive samples from sender
- Verify sender identity
- Verify sample integrity
- Verify sample quantity

**2. Registration:**
- Register samples in log
- Assign unique sample number
- Record receipt date
- Record sample information

**3. Inspection:**
- Inspect samples for integrity
- Inspect samples for quantity
- Inspect samples for quality
- Inspect samples for packaging

**4. Acceptance or Rejection:**
- Accept samples if suitable
- Reject samples if not suitable
- Notify sender of decision
- Document decision

### Sample Storage Procedures (9:00 - 13:00)
**Speaker:** Sample storage must follow specific procedures.

**Procedures:**

**1. Initial Storage:**
- Store samples in appropriate location
- Store samples in appropriate conditions
- Store samples by type
- Store samples by sensitivity

**2. Long-term Storage:**
- Store samples for specified period
- Store samples in appropriate conditions
- Monitor samples regularly
- Determine expiration date

**3. Special Storage:**
- Store special samples separately
- Store hazardous samples separately
- Store sensitive samples separately
- Store perishable samples separately

**4. Protection:**
- Protect samples from damage
- Protect samples from contamination
- Protect samples from heat
- Protect samples from humidity

### Analysis Procedures (13:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Sample analysis must follow specific procedures.

**Procedures:**

**1. Sample Preparation:**
- Prepare sample for analysis
- Dilute sample
- Purify sample
- Store sample

**2. Analysis:**
- Perform analysis according to method
- Use appropriate equipment
- Follow procedures
- Monitor process

**3. Data Collection:**
- Collect data accurately
- Record data
- Analyze data
- Interpret data

**4. Disposal:**
- Dispose of samples correctly
- Dispose of samples safely
- Dispose of samples according to regulations
- Document disposal

- Analysis procedures
