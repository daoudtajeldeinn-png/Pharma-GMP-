# Video Script: Validation - Unit 2.3
## Cleaning Validation
**Duration:** 20 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss Cleaning Validation. Cleaning Validation is essential to ensure that cleaning is effective and prevents cross-contamination.

### What is Cleaning Validation? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Cleaning Validation is the process of demonstrating that cleaning procedures are effective in removing contaminants.

**Definition:**
- Demonstrating that cleaning is effective
- Demonstrating that contaminants are completely removed
- Demonstrating that no contaminant residues remain
- Demonstrating that cleaning is consistent

**Importance of Cleaning Validation:**
- Prevents cross-contamination
- Ensures product quality
- Ensures patient safety
- Essential for regulatory compliance

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Cleaning Validation is essential for equipment used for multiple products
- Cleaning Validation must be performed before use
- Cleaning Validation must be documented

### Cleaning Validation Principles (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Cleaning Validation must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Identification:** Identify target contaminants
2. **Selection:** Select appropriate cleaning method
3. **Verification:** Verify cleaning effectiveness
4. **Documentation:** Complete documentation
5. **Monitoring:** Continuous monitoring

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Accurate contaminant identification is essential
- Selecting appropriate method is essential
- Verifying effectiveness is essential
- Complete documentation is essential
- Continuous monitoring is essential

### Cleaning Validation Stages (9:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Cleaning Validation includes several organized stages.

**Stages:**

**1. Identify Contaminants:**
- Identify previous products
- Identify chemicals
- Identify biological materials
- Identify other contaminants

**2. Develop Cleaning Procedures:**
- Select cleaning agents
- Define cleaning steps
- Define parameters
- Define frequency

**3. Execute Cleaning:**
- Execute cleaning procedures
- Monitor execution
- Record data
- Address problems

**4. Verify Effectiveness:**
- Test residues
- Analyze results
- Evaluate effectiveness
- Identify corrective actions

**5. Documentation:**
- Document all activities
- Prepare report
- Review report
- Approve report

### Residue Testing Methods (15:00 - 20:00)
**Speaker:** There are several methods for testing residues after cleaning.

**Main Methods:**

**1. TOC (Total Organic Carbon) Test:**
- Detects organic residues
- Very sensitive
- Fast
- Suitable for most contaminants

**2. Conductivity Test:**
- Detects ionic residues
- Fast and easy
- Suitable for ionic materials

**3. pH Test:**
- Detects acidic or alkaline residues
- Fast and easy
- Suitable for acidic or alkaline materials

**4. UV-Vis Test:**
- Detects specific residues
- Sensitive and specific
- Suitable for specific materials

**5. HPLC Test:**
- Detects specific residues
- Very sensitive and specific
- Suitable for active materials

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Selecting appropriate method depends on contaminant
- May require using multiple methods together
- Acceptable limits must be defined in advance
