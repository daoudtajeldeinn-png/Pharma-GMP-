# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 5.3
## Risk Management
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss risk management. Risk management is essential to identify, evaluate, and control risks associated with products and processes.

### What is Risk Management? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Risk management is an organized process to identify, evaluate, and control risks.

**Definition:**
- Identifying potential risks
- Evaluating probability and severity
- Controlling risks
- Monitoring control effectiveness

**Importance of Risk Management:**
- Protecting patients
- Protecting the company
- Regulatory compliance
- Continuous improvement

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Risk management is an integral part of GMP
- Risk management must be continuous
- Risk management requires participation from all employees

### Risk Management Principles (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Risk management must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Scientific Evaluation:** Using scientific methods
2. **Transparency:** Open communication
3. **Participation:** Involving all stakeholders
4. **Continuous Improvement:** Continuous updates
5. **Proportionality:** Control proportional to risks

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Scientific evaluation ensures accuracy
- Transparency builds trust
- Participation ensures comprehensive perspectives
- Continuous improvement ensures relevance
- Proportionality ensures efficiency

### Risk Management Procedures (9:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** Risk management includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Risk Identification:**
- Identify all potential risks
- Use tools like Brainstorming
- Review historical data
- Consult experts

**2. Risk Assessment:**
- Evaluate probability of risks occurring
- Evaluate severity of risks
- Determine risk priority
- Use risk matrix

**3. Risk Control:**
- Develop control procedures
- Implement procedures
- Evaluate procedure effectiveness
- Update control procedures

**4. Risk Monitoring:**
- Monitor control effectiveness
- Monitor new risks
- Update risk assessment
- Continuous communication

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Risk identification must be comprehensive
- Risk assessment must be objective
- Risk control must be effective
- Risk monitoring must be continuous

### Risk Management Tools (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** There are several tools that can be used for risk management.

**Main Tools:**

**1. FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis):**
- Analysis of failure modes and effects
- Identify potential failure points
- Evaluate severity, frequency, and detection of failure
- Develop control procedures

**2. FTA (Fault Tree Analysis):**
- Fault tree analysis
- Identify root causes
- Evaluate failure probability
- Develop preventive procedures

**3. HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points):**
- Hazard analysis and critical control points
- Identify critical control points
- Develop critical limits
- Monitor critical points

**4. Risk Matrix:**
- Risk matrix
- Classify risks by probability and severity
- Determine risk priority
- Guide decisions

**Detailed Explanation:**
- FMEA is suitable for complex processes
- FTA is suitable for root cause analysis
- HACCP is suitable for food and pharmaceutical processes
- Risk Matrix is suitable for quick evaluation

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- What is risk management
- Risk management principles
- Risk management procedures
- Risk management tools

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Risk management is essential for GMP
- Risk management must be continuous
- There are several tools for risk management
- Participation from all employees is essential
