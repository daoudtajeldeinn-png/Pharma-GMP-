What is Risk Management?
(1:00 - 5:00) Risk management is the process of identifying, evaluating, and managing potential risks.
Identify potential risks Evaluate probability and severity Develop control procedures Monitor risks continuously of Risk Management: Prevents potential problems Reduces damages Improves quality Essential for compliance Risk management must be comprehensive Risk management must be continuous Risk management must be documented Risk management must be followed up Types of Risks in Laboratory (5:00 - 10:00) There are several types of risks in the laboratory.
Quality Risks: Inaccurate results Unreliable results Inconsistent results Unacceptable results Safety Risks: Employee injuries Laboratory damage Fires Explosions Compliance Risks: Non-compliance with regulations Fines Loss of accreditation Loss of trust Operational Risks: Errors in operations Delays in operations Failures in operations Recurring problems Equipment Risks: Equipment failure Incorrect calibration Insufficient maintenance Incorrect use Risk Management Procedures (10:00 - 14:00) Risk management requires following specific procedures.
Identify Risks: Identify all potential risks Identify risk sources Identify risk impact Identify risk probability Evaluate Risks: Evaluate probability of occurrence Evaluate severity of impact Determine risk level Determine priorities Address Risks: Develop control procedures Develop preventive procedures Develop response procedures Develop emergency plans Monitor Risks: Monitor risks continuously Monitor procedure effectiveness Monitor changes in risks Update evaluations Risk Management Tools (14:00 - 18:00) There are several tools that can be used for risk management.
Main Tools: FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis): Identify potential failure modes Identify failure effects Determine priorities Develop control procedures Risk Matrix: Determine probability of occurrence Determine severity of impact Determine risk level Determine priorities HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points): Identify hazards Identify critical control points Develop control procedures Monitor critical points SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats): Identify strengths Identify weaknesses Identify opportunities Identify threats (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: What is risk management Types of risks in laboratory Risk management procedures Risk management tools Key Points to Remember: Risk management is essential Risks must be identified Risks must be managed Tools help in management In the next lecture, we will discuss QC Lab case studies.
