# Video Script: ISO 9001 - Unit 2.1
## Context of the Organization
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss the context of the organization. Understanding the organization context is essential to effectively apply the quality management system.

### What is Organization Context? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Organization context is the environment in which the organization operates.

**Definition:**
- Internal and external environment
- Factors that affect the organization
- Factors that affect the quality management system
- Factors that affect the organization's ability to achieve objectives

**Importance of Understanding Context:**
- Helps identify risks and opportunities
- Helps identify stakeholder needs
- Helps define quality management system scope
- Helps improve performance

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Context includes internal and external factors
- Context includes positive and negative factors
- Context changes continuously
- Context must be reviewed regularly

### Internal Factors (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** Internal factors are factors that the organization controls.

**Main Factors:**

**1. Organizational Culture:**
- Values and principles
- Beliefs and behaviors
- Management style
- Work environment

**2. Performance:**
- Current performance
- Past performance
- Performance objectives
- Performance metrics

**3. Resources:**
- Human resources
- Financial resources
- Technical resources
- Physical resources

**4. Processes:**
- Current processes
- Process efficiency
- Process effectiveness
- Process integration

**5. Structure:**
- Organizational structure
- Roles and responsibilities
- Reporting lines
- Work relationships

### External Factors (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** External factors are factors that the organization does not control.

**Main Factors:**

**1. Legal Environment:**
- Regulations and laws
- Regulatory standards
- Compliance requirements
- Legal changes

**2. Economic Environment:**
- Economic conditions
- Inflation
- Exchange rates
- Competition

**3. Technological Environment:**
- Technological developments
- Innovations
- New technology
- Cyber threats

**4. Social Environment:**
- Demographic changes
- Cultural changes
- Societal expectations
- Social responsibility

**5. Environmental Environment:**
- Climate change
- Sustainability
- Environmental protection
- Environmental requirements

### Context Analysis (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Context analysis is essential to understand factors and their impact.

**Steps:**

**1. Identify Factors:**
- Identify internal factors
- Identify external factors
- Identify positive factors
- Identify negative factors

**2. Evaluate Impact:**
- Evaluate impact of each factor
- Evaluate probability of occurrence
- Evaluate severity of impact
- Determine priorities

**3. Identify Risks and Opportunities:**
- Identify potential risks
- Identify potential opportunities
- Evaluate risks and opportunities
- Develop strategies

**4. Review Context:**
- Review context regularly
- Update evaluation
- Update strategies
- Document

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- What is organization context
- Internal factors
- External factors
- Context analysis

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Context includes internal and external factors
- Understanding context is essential
- Context analysis is essential
- Continuous review is essential

In the next lecture, we will discuss Stakeholders.
