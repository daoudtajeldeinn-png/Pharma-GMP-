Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 3 Batch Records (BMR/BPR) Duration: 22 minutes (0:00 - 1:00) In this lecture, we will discuss batch records (BMR/BPR).
Batch records are essential to track the production of each batch and ensure its quality.
What Are Batch Records?
(1:00 - 5:00) Batch records are documents used to record all details of producing a specific batch.
BMR = Batch Manufacturing Record BPR = Batch Production Record Both refer to the same document Purpose: Track production of each batch Ensure all processes are executed correctly Provide proof of quality Provide a record for traceability Each batch has its own record Record must be filled during production Record must be signed by responsible personnel Record must be kept for review Batch Record Contents (5:00 - 12:00) The batch record contains comprehensive information about the batch.
Main Sections: Batch Information: Batch number Product name Batch size Production date Expiration date Raw Material Information: Raw material numbers used Raw material quantities Raw material batch numbers Raw material receipt dates Equipment Used: Equipment numbers Equipment status Maintenance schedules Calibration schedules Operating Procedures followed SOP numbers Any deviations from procedures Process Parameters: Temperature Pressure Time Speed Any other parameters In-Process Quality Tests: Test results Test times Any deviations Corrective actions Personnel: Employee names Employee signatures Attendance times Responsibilities Documentation: All observations Any problems Any corrective actions Final signature Preparing Batch Record (12:00 - 16:00) Preparing the batch record must be done correctly to ensure information accuracy.
Steps: Review Formula: Ensure formula is current Ensure formula is approved Ensure formula is suitable for the product Prepare Form: Use approved form Include all required sections Ensure form clarity Review Raw Materials: Ensure raw material availability Ensure raw material quality Ensure raw material validity Review Equipment: Ensure equipment readiness Ensure equipment maintenance Ensure equipment calibration Review Personnel: Ensure personnel availability Ensure personnel training Ensure personnel competency Using Batch Record (16:00 - 20:00) Using the batch record during production is essential to ensure recording accuracy.
Before Production: Review batch record Ensure information completeness Sign to start During Production: Record all parameters Record all tests Record any deviations Sign each step After Production: Review record Ensure completeness Final signature Archive record Recording must be in real-time Recording must be accurate Recording must be legible Recording must be indelible Reviewing Batch Record (20:00 - 22:00) Reviewing the batch record is essential to ensure information accuracy and compliance.
Reviews: Self-Review: By employees Supervisor Review: By direct supervisor Quality Review: By quality department Final Review: By management What is Reviewed: Completeness of all sections Information accuracy Signatures Any deviations Corrective actions Review must be comprehensive Any errors must be corrected Corrections must be documented Record must be approved before release (22:00) In this lecture, we learned: What are batch records Batch record contents Preparing batch record Using batch record Reviewing batch record Key Points to Remember: Batch records are essential for tracking production Record contains comprehensive information Record must be filled during production Review is essential for release In the next lecture, we will discuss change control.
