# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 1.3
## Differences Between GMP, GCP, and GLP
**Duration:** 12 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will learn about the differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP. These three practices together form the quality framework in the pharmaceutical industry, but each focuses on a different stage.

### GMP - Good Manufacturing Practice (1:00 - 4:00)
**Speaker:** GMP, as we learned, focuses on the manufacturing stage.

**Scope:**
- Drug manufacturing
- Quality control during manufacturing
- Storage and distribution
- Facility and equipment management

**Objective:**
- Ensure product quality consistency
- Prevent contamination and errors
- Ensure regulatory compliance

**Examples:**
- Manufacturing tablets and capsules
- Filling liquid medications
- Storing raw materials and finished products

### GCP - Good Clinical Practice (4:00 - 7:00)
**Speaker:** GCP focuses on the clinical trial stage.

**Scope:**
- Designing clinical trials
- Conducting clinical trials
- Collecting and analyzing data
- Protecting participants' rights

**Objective:**
- Ensure participant safety in trials
- Collect reliable data
- Protect human rights

**Examples:**
- Phase I trials
- Phase II/III trials
- Bioavailability studies

**Detailed Explanation:**
GCP includes:
- Ethical approval
- Participant selection
- Trial monitoring
- Report preparation

### GLP - Good Laboratory Practice (7:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** GLP focuses on the preclinical research and development stage.

**Scope:**
- Preclinical studies
- Toxicity testing
- Efficacy testing
- Sample analysis

**Objective:**
- Ensure preclinical data quality
- Minimize laboratory errors
- Ensure result consistency

**Examples:**
- Toxicity testing on animals
- Stability studies
- Efficacy testing in laboratory

**Detailed Explanation:**
GLP includes:
- Laboratory management
- Employee training
- Equipment maintenance
- Document management

### Relationship Between the Three (10:00 - 12:00)
**Speaker:** GMP, GCP, and GLP complement each other.

**Timeline:**
1. GLP - Preclinical research and development
2. GCP - Clinical trials
3. GMP - Manufacturing and marketing

**Integration:**
- All ensure drug quality and safety
- All require documentation and compliance
- All aim to protect patients

**Detailed Explanation:**
Drug lifecycle:
1. Laboratory research (GLP)
2. Clinical trials (GCP)
3. Manufacturing (GMP)
4. Marketing and monitoring (GMP + GCP)

### Summary (12:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP
- Scope of each practice
- How the three integrate together

**Key Points to Remember:**
- GMP = Manufacturing
- GCP = Clinical trials
- GLP = Preclinical research
- All are essential for drug quality

In the next lecture, we will discuss the manufacturing environment and facility design.
