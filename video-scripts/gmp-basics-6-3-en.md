# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 6.3
## Complaints and Recalls
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss complaints and recalls. Managing complaints and recalls is essential to protect patients and company reputation.

### What Are Complaints? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** A complaint is any contact from patients, doctors, or pharmacists reporting a problem with the product.

**Definition:**
- Any contact reporting a problem
- May be from patients, doctors, or pharmacists
- May be about quality, safety, or efficacy
- Must be addressed quickly

**Types of Complaints:**
1. **Quality Complaints:** Product quality problems
2. **Safety Complaints:** Product safety problems
3. **Efficacy Complaints:** Product efficacy problems
4. **Other Complaints:** Packaging or labeling problems

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Complaints must be recorded immediately
- Complaints must be investigated
- Complaints must be addressed quickly

### Complaint Management Principles (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Complaint management must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Rapid Response:** Respond to complaints quickly
2. **Comprehensive Investigation:** Investigate all complaints
3. **Complete Documentation:** Document all activities
4. **Corrective Actions:** Develop corrective actions when necessary
5. **Communication:** Communicate with complainant

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Rapid response builds trust
- Comprehensive investigation identifies cause
- Documentation provides a record for traceability
- Corrective actions prevent recurrence
- Communication shows concern

### Complaint Management Procedures (9:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** Complaint management includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Receipt:**
- Record complaint immediately
- Assign complaint number
- Notify management
- Acknowledge receipt

**2. Initial Assessment:**
- Evaluate complaint seriousness
- Determine if immediate action is required
- Determine responsible team
- Determine timeline

**3. Investigation:**
- Collect information
- Review records
- Examine affected product
- Interview concerned employees

**4. Reporting:**
- Prepare complaint report
- Identify root cause
- Identify corrective actions
- Approve report

**5. Response:**
- Respond to complainant
- Explain actions taken
- Apologize when necessary
- Provide compensation when necessary

**6. Follow-up:**
- Monitor action effectiveness
- Communicate with complainant
- Update procedures
- Document

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Immediate recording is essential
- Initial assessment determines priority
- Investigation must be comprehensive
- Response must be quick
- Follow-up is essential to ensure satisfaction

### What is a Recall? (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** A recall is the removal of product from the market due to quality or safety problems.

**Definition:**
- Removing product from market
- May be voluntary or mandatory
- May be partial or complete
- Essential to protect patients

**Types of Recalls:**
1. **Voluntary Recall:** From the company itself
2. **Mandatory Recall:** From regulatory authority
3. **Partial Recall:** For a specific batch
4. **Complete Recall:** For all batches

**Recall Procedures:**
1. Identify affected product
2. Notify regulatory authorities
3. Notify distributors and pharmacists
4. Notify patients
5. Retrieve product
6. Investigate cause
7. Develop corrective actions

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Recall must be quick
- Recall must be comprehensive
- Recall must be documented
- Recall requires cooperation from all parties

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- What are complaints
- Complaint management principles
- Complaint management procedures
- What is a recall

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Complaints must be addressed quickly
- Comprehensive investigation is essential
- Recall is essential to protect patients
- Documentation is essential for review
