# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 1.2
## Main Regulatory Authorities
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will learn about the main regulatory authorities that establish and implement GMP standards. These agencies play a vital role in ensuring the quality and safety of pharmaceuticals.

### FDA - US Food and Drug Administration (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** FDA is the US Food and Drug Administration, one of the oldest and most important regulatory agencies in the world.

**Key Points:**
- Established in 1906
- Responsible for regulating drugs in the United States
- Issues GMP regulations (21 CFR Parts 210/211)
- Conducts inspections and monitoring

**FDA GMP Regulations:**
- Part 210: Good Manufacturing Practice for drugs
- Part 211: Good Manufacturing Practice for finished pharmaceuticals

**Detailed Explanation:**
FDA applies strict standards to:
- US manufacturing facilities
- Facilities that export to the US
- Companies seeking approval for new products

### EMA - European Medicines Agency (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** EMA is the European Medicines Agency, responsible for regulating drugs in the European Union.

**Key Points:**
- Established in 1995
- Headquarters in London (currently Amsterdam)
- Coordinates between national regulatory authorities in Europe
- Issues EU GMP guidelines

**EU GMP Guidelines:**
- Volume 1: Good Manufacturing Practice for medicinal products
- Volume 2: Good Manufacturing Practice for starting materials
- Annexes: Specific guidelines (such as Annex 1 for sterile products)

**Detailed Explanation:**
EMA focuses on:
- Harmonizing standards in Europe
- Evaluating marketing authorization applications
- Post-marketing surveillance of medicines

### WHO - World Health Organization (9:00 - 12:00)
**Speaker:** WHO is the World Health Organization, an international organization that sets GMP standards for developing countries.

**Key Points:**
- Established in 1948
- Headquarters in Geneva
- Issues international GMP guidelines
- Helps countries implement GMP standards

**WHO GMP Guidelines:**
- GMP guidelines for active pharmaceutical ingredients
- GMP guidelines for starting materials
- GMP guidelines for sterile products

**Detailed Explanation:**
WHO focuses on:
- Technical assistance to developing countries
- International standard harmonization
- Improving drug quality globally

### SFDA - Saudi Food and Drug Authority (12:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** SFDA is the Saudi Food and Drug Authority, responsible for regulating drugs in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

**Key Points:**
- Established in 2003
- Headquarters in Riyadh
- Issues Saudi GMP regulations
- Conducts inspections and monitoring

**SFDA GMP Regulations:**
- Aligned with ICH and WHO standards
- Tailored to the Saudi environment
- Requires compliance from all companies

**Detailed Explanation:**
SFDA focuses on:
- Protecting public health in Saudi Arabia
- Ensuring quality of imported and locally manufactured drugs
- Coordination with international agencies

### Differences Between Agencies (15:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Although all these agencies aim for the same goal, there are differences:

**Main Differences:**
- Geographic scope
- Technical details
- Inspection procedures
- Documentation requirements

**Similarities:**
- All focus on patient safety
- All implement GMP standards
- All require strict compliance

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned about:
- US FDA
- European EMA
- Global WHO
- Saudi SFDA

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Each agency has its geographic scope
- All implement GMP standards
- Compliance is required for each agency

In the next lecture, we will discuss the differences between GMP, GCP, and GLP.
