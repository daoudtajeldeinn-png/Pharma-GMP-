# Video Script: Validation - Unit 5.2
## Revalidation
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss Revalidation. Revalidation is essential to ensure continuity of validation state after changes or over time.

### What is Revalidation? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Revalidation is the re-verification of the system or process after changes or over time.

**Definition:**
- Re-verification of the system
- Re-verification of the process
- Re-verification of equipment
- Ensure quality continuity

**Importance of Revalidation:**
- Ensures continuity of validation state
- Ensures changes do not have negative effects
- Ensures regulatory compliance
- Essential for continuous quality

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Revalidation is essential after significant changes
- Revalidation is essential periodically
- Revalidation must be documented

### When is Revalidation Required? (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** There are several situations that require revalidation.

**Main Situations:**

**1. After Significant Changes:**
- Equipment change
- Process change
- Raw material change
- Site change

**2. After a Specific Time Period:**
- Periodic revalidation
- Usually every 1-3 years
- Depends on system type
- Depends on usage

**3. After Problems:**
- After deviations
- After quality failures
- After complaints
- After recalls

**4. After Improvements:**
- After process improvement
- After equipment update
- After software update
- After procedure update

### Types of Revalidation (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of revalidation, each serving a different purpose.

**Main Types:**

**1. Full Revalidation:**
- Re-execute all validation stages
- Essential after large changes
- Requires DQ, IQ, OQ, PQ
- Costly but comprehensive

**2. Partial Revalidation:**
- Re-execute specific stages
- Essential after small changes
- Requires only affected stages
- Less costly and faster

**3. Periodic Revalidation:**
- Re-validate periodically
- Essential to maintain validation state
- Based on risk assessment
- Usually every 1-3 years

**4. Continued Process Verification:**
- Continuous performance monitoring
- Essential for continuous processes
- Requires monitoring and analysis
- Part of CPV

### Revalidation Procedures (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Executing revalidation requires following specific procedures.

**Steps:**

**1. Need Assessment:**
- Evaluate if revalidation is required
- Determine type of revalidation required
- Define scope
- Identify resources

**2. Plan Development:**
- Develop revalidation plan
- Define required stages
- Define timeline
- Identify resources

**3. Revalidation Execution:**
- Execute required stages
- Collect data
- Analyze results
- Address any problems

**4. Documentation:**
- Document all activities
- Prepare revalidation report
- Review report
- Approve report

**5. Validation Status Update:**
- Update validation status
- Update records
- Notify employees
- Notify regulatory authorities when necessary

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- What is Revalidation
- When is Revalidation required
- Types of Revalidation
- Revalidation procedures

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Revalidation is essential for validation continuity
- Revalidation is required after changes
- Revalidation is required periodically
- Documentation is essential for review
