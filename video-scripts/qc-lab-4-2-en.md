# Video Script: QC Lab & ISO/IEC 17025 - Unit 4.2
## Risk Management
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss risk management in the laboratory. Risk management is essential to prevent problems and ensure quality.

### What is Risk Management? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Risk management is the process of identifying, evaluating, and managing potential risks.

**Definition:**
- Identify potential risks
- Evaluate probability and severity
- Develop control procedures
- Monitor risks continuously

**Importance of Risk Management:**
- Prevents potential problems
- Reduces damages
- Improves quality
- Essential for compliance

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Risk management must be comprehensive
- Risk management must be continuous
- Risk management must be documented
- Risk management must be followed up

### Types of Risks in Laboratory (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of risks in the laboratory.

**Main Types:**

**1. Quality Risks:**
- Inaccurate results
- Unreliable results
- Inconsistent results
- Unacceptable results

**2. Safety Risks:**
- Employee injuries
- Laboratory damage
- Fires
- Explosions

**3. Compliance Risks:**
- Non-compliance with regulations
- Fines
- Loss of accreditation
- Loss of trust

**4. Operational Risks:**
- Errors in operations
- Delays in operations
- Failures in operations
- Recurring problems

**5. Equipment Risks:**
- Equipment failure
- Incorrect calibration
- Insufficient maintenance
- Incorrect use

### Risk Management Procedures (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** Risk management requires following specific procedures.

**Procedures:**

**1. Identify Risks:**
- Identify all potential risks
- Identify risk sources
- Identify risk impact
- Identify risk probability

**2. Evaluate Risks:**
- Evaluate probability of occurrence
- Evaluate severity of impact
- Determine risk level
- Determine priorities

**3. Address Risks:**
- Develop control procedures
- Develop preventive procedures
- Develop response procedures
- Develop emergency plans

**4. Monitor Risks:**
- Monitor risks continuously
- Monitor procedure effectiveness
- Monitor changes in risks
- Update evaluations

### Risk Management Tools (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** There are several tools that can be used for risk management.

**Main Tools:**

**1. FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis):**
- Identify potential failure modes
- Identify failure effects
- Determine priorities
- Develop control procedures

**2. Risk Matrix:**
- Determine probability of occurrence
- Determine severity of impact
- Determine risk level
- Determine priorities

**3. HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points):**
- Identify hazards
- Identify critical control points
- Develop control procedures
- Monitor critical points

**4. SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats):**
- Identify strengths
- Identify weaknesses
- Identify opportunities
- Identify threats

- Risk management procedures
- Risk management tools

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Risk management is essential
- Risks must be identified

- Tools help in management
