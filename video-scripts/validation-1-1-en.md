# Video Script: Validation - Unit 1.1
## Definition and Types of Validation
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** Welcome to this lecture. Today we will discuss the definition of validation and the different types of validation in the pharmaceutical industry.

### What is Validation? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Validation is the process of demonstrating with evidence that any process, system, or activity consistently and reliably produces the required results.

**Definition:**
- Demonstration with scientific evidence
- Ensuring consistency and quality
- Regulatory compliance
- Risk reduction

**Difference Between Verification and Validation:**
- Verification: Did we build the system correctly?
- Validation: Did we build the right system?

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Verification ensures the system is built according to specifications
- Validation ensures the system achieves the intended purpose
- Both are essential for quality

### Types of Validation (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of validation in the pharmaceutical industry, each serving a different purpose.

**Main Types:**

**1. Prospective Validation:**
- Performed before starting commercial production
- Used for new processes and equipment
- Requires detailed protocol
- Requires execution of 3 successful batches

**2. Concurrent Validation:**
- Performed during commercial production
- Used when production cannot be stopped
- Requires intensive monitoring
- Requires contingency procedures

**3. Retrospective Validation:**
- Performed after commercial production
- Used for existing processes
- Relies on historical data
- Requires comprehensive data analysis

**4. Revalidation:**
- Performed after significant changes
- Includes evaluation of changes
- May require execution of new batches
- Essential to maintain validated state

### Validation Principles (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** Validation must follow specific principles to ensure effectiveness.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Planning:** Comprehensive planning before starting
2. **Execution:** Execution according to protocol
3. **Documentation:** Complete documentation of activities
4. **Review:** Reviewing results
5. **Maintenance:** Continuous maintenance of validated state

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Planning determines scope and approach
- Execution follows approved protocol
- Documentation provides compliance evidence
- Review ensures effectiveness
- Maintenance ensures quality continuity

### Regulatory Requirements (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Validation is required by various regulatory authorities.

**Main Regulations:**
1. **FDA 21 CFR Part 211:** US FDA regulations
2. **EU GMP Annex 15:** European Union guidelines
3. **WHO GMP:** World Health Organization guidelines
4. **ICH Q7:** ICH guidelines for raw materials

**Detailed Explanation:**
- FDA requires validation for all critical processes
- EU GMP requires validation for equipment and processes
- WHO provides guidelines for developing countries
- ICH Q7 focuses on raw materials

**Compliance:**
- Validation is essential for obtaining licenses
- Validation is essential for regulatory inspection
- Validation is essential for product export

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Definition of validation
- Difference between verification and validation
- Different types of validation
- Validation principles
- Regulatory requirements

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Validation is demonstration with evidence
- There are several types of validation
- Validation must follow specific principles
- Validation is required by regulations
