# Video Script: GMP Basics - Unit 6.1
## Internal Inspection
**Duration:** 20 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss internal inspection. Internal inspection is essential to ensure compliance with GMP standards and identify areas for improvement.

### What is Internal Inspection? (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Internal inspection is a systematic and independent examination of activities to determine the extent of compliance with requirements.

**Definition:**
- Systematic examination of activities
- Evaluation of GMP compliance
- Identification of improvement areas
- Providing evidence to management

**Importance of Internal Inspection:**
- Ensuring regulatory compliance
- Identifying problems before external inspection
- Improving processes
- Providing opportunity for continuous improvement

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Internal inspection must be independent
- Internal inspection must be objective
- Internal inspection must be documented

### Internal Inspection Principles (5:00 - 9:00)
**Speaker:** Internal inspection must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Independence:** The auditor must be independent
2. **Objectivity:** Inspection must be objective
3. **Comprehensiveness:** Inspection must be comprehensive
4. **Documentation:** All activities must be documented
5. **Follow-up:** Corrective actions must be followed up

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Independence ensures neutrality
- Objectivity ensures accuracy
- Comprehensiveness ensures completeness
- Documentation provides evidence
- Follow-up ensures effectiveness

### Internal Inspection Procedures (9:00 - 15:00)
**Speaker:** Internal inspection includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Planning:**
- Determine scope
- Determine team
- Develop inspection plan
- Notify employees

**2. Execution:**
- Review documents
- Observe processes
- Interview employees
- Collect evidence

**3. Reporting:**
- Analyze results
- Prepare inspection report
- Discuss results with management
- Identify corrective actions

**4. Follow-up:**
- Monitor implementation of corrective actions
- Evaluate action effectiveness
- Re-inspect when necessary
- Document

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Planning must be detailed
- Execution must follow the plan
- Reporting must be accurate
- Follow-up is essential to ensure effectiveness

### Inspection Areas (15:00 - 20:00)
**Speaker:** Internal inspection must cover all GMP areas.

**Main Areas:**

**1. Facilities and Equipment:**
- Facility design
- Equipment maintenance
- Equipment calibration
- Cleaning and sterilization

**2. Personnel:**
- Training
- Health and hygiene
- Behavior in clean areas
- Roles and responsibilities

**3. Documents:**
- Document management
- Batch records
- SOPs
- Records

**4. Processes:**
- Manufacturing procedures
- Quality control
- Change control
- Risk management

**5. Quality:**
- Quality tests
- Deviation management
- Complaints and recalls
- Continuous improvement

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Each area must be examined in detail
- Evidence must be collected
- Deviations must be documented
- Corrective actions must be identified

### Summary (20:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- What is internal inspection
- Internal inspection principles
- Internal inspection procedures
- Inspection areas

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Internal inspection is essential for GMP
- Internal inspection must be independent
- Internal inspection must be comprehensive
- Follow-up is essential to ensure effectiveness

In the next lecture, we will discuss deviations and investigation.
