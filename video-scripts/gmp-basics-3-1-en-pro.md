of Personnel in GMP (1:00 - 4:00) Personnel are the most important element in the GMP system.

Why Are Personnel Important?
They execute the processes They discover problems They document activities They maintain quality Every employee must be trained Training must be continuous Competence must be evaluated regularly Records must be kept Types of Training (4:00 - 8:00) There are several types of training, each serving a different purpose.
Orientation Training: For new employees Basic GMP Training: For all employees Job-Specific Training: According to the job SOP Training: For Standard Operating Procedures Advanced Training: For experienced employees Refresher Training: Periodic for Each Type: Orientation Training: to the company to GMP Company policies Safety procedures Basic GMP Training: GMP principles Regulatory requirements of quality Employee responsibilities Job-Specific Training: Equipment usage Process execution Document management Emergency procedures SOP Training: Reading and understanding SOPs Correct SOP execution Documentation Deviation handling Refresher Training: Knowledge update Reviewing changes Skill improvement Competence evaluation Training Program (8:00 - 12:00) A good training program must be planned and organized.
Training Program Elements: Needs Assessment: Determining what each employee needs Content Development: Creating appropriate training materials Training Delivery: Effective training delivery Effectiveness Evaluation: Measuring training results Continuous Improvement: Regularly updating the program Needs Assessment: Job analysis Identifying required skills Evaluating current competence Identifying gaps Content Development: Using current materials Including practical examples Using visual aids Ensuring ease of understanding Training Delivery: Using qualified trainers Providing appropriate learning environment Encouraging participation Providing reference materials Effectiveness Evaluation: Post-training tests Performance monitoring Supervisor feedback Self-evaluation Training Records (12:00 - 15:00) Training records are essential for review and regulatory compliance.
Training Record Contents: Employee name Training date Training topic Trainer name Training duration Evaluation results Employee and trainer signatures Record Management: Store records centrally Easy access to records Keep records for a specified period Protect records from damage Records must be up-to-date Records must be accurate Records must be traceable Records must be available for inspection Competence Evaluation (15:00 - 18:00) Competence evaluation is essential to ensure employees are capable of correctly performing their tasks.
Competence Evaluation Methods: Direct Observation: Monitoring employee during work Tests: Written or practical tests Interviews: Discussion with employee Record Review: Reviewing performance records Peer Evaluation: Evaluation by colleagues Evaluation must be fair Evaluation must be continuous Evaluation must be documented Evaluation must be used to improve performance (18:00) In this lecture, we learned: of personnel in GMP Different types of training Training program Training records Competence evaluation Key Points to Remember: Personnel are the foundation of GMP Training must be continuous Records are essential for review Competence evaluation is essential In the next lecture, we will discuss health and personal hygiene.
