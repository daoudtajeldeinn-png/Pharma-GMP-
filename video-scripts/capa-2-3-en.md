# Video Script: CAPA - Unit 2.3
## Developing Preventive Actions
**Duration:** 18 minutes

---

### Introduction (0:00 - 1:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we will discuss developing preventive actions. Preventive actions are essential to prevent future problems.

### Principles of Developing Preventive Actions (1:00 - 5:00)
**Speaker:** Developing preventive actions must follow specific principles.

**Basic Principles:**
1. **Proactiveness:** Actions must be proactive
2. **Effectiveness:** Actions must be effective
3. **Realism:** Actions must be realistic
4. **Appropriateness:** Actions must be appropriate
5. **Timeline:** Actions must have a timeline

**Detailed Explanation:**
- Preventive actions are taken before the problem occurs
- Preventive actions are based on risk analysis
- Preventive actions are based on historical data
- Preventive actions are based on trends

### Steps for Developing Preventive Actions (5:00 - 10:00)
**Speaker:** Developing preventive actions includes several organized steps.

**Steps:**

**1. Identify Potential Risks:**
- Identify potential risks
- Identify risk sources
- Identify risk impact
- Identify risk probability

**2. Evaluate Risks:**
- Evaluate probability of occurrence
- Evaluate severity of impact
- Determine risk level
- Determine priorities

**3. Develop Actions:**
- Develop actions to limit risks
- Develop actions to control risks
- Develop actions to eliminate risks
- Define required resources

**4. Review Actions:**
- Review actions by experts
- Review actions by quality
- Review actions by management
- Required adjustments

**5. Approve Actions:**
- Approval from quality
- Approval from management
- Documentation
- Signature

### Types of Preventive Actions (10:00 - 14:00)
**Speaker:** There are several types of preventive actions.

**Main Types:**

**1. Preventive Maintenance Actions:**
- Periodic equipment maintenance
- Preventive equipment maintenance
- Periodic device calibration
- Periodic facility cleaning

**2. Preventive Training Actions:**
- Periodic employee training
- Training on potential risks
- Training on new procedures
- Training on new skills

**3. Preventive Monitoring Actions:**
- Continuous performance monitoring
- Continuous quality monitoring
- Continuous trend monitoring
- Continuous risk monitoring

**4. Preventive Improvement Actions:**
- Continuous process improvement
- Continuous quality improvement
- Continuous efficiency improvement
- Continuous effectiveness improvement

### Examples of Preventive Actions (14:00 - 18:00)
**Speaker:** Let's look at some practical examples.

**Example 1: Preventing Equipment Failure**

**Preventive Actions:**
- Periodic preventive maintenance
- Periodic calibration
- Performance monitoring
- Employee training on maintenance

**Example 2: Preventing Employee Errors**

**Preventive Actions:**
- Periodic training
- Update SOPs
- Performance monitoring
- Reward good performance

**Example 3: Preventing Product Contamination**

**Preventive Actions:**
- Improve cleaning procedures
- Improve sterilization procedures
- Environment monitoring
- Improve health and hygiene procedures

### Summary (18:00)
**Speaker:** In this lecture, we learned:
- Principles of developing preventive actions
- Steps for developing preventive actions
- Types of preventive actions
- Examples of preventive actions

**Key Points to Remember:**
- Preventive actions are essential
- Preventive actions are based on risk analysis
- Preventive actions prevent future problems
- Preventive actions reduce costs
